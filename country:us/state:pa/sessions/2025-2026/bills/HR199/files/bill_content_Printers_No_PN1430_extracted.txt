WILLIAMS, O'MARA, MENTZER, K.HARRIS, CONKLIN, CIRESI, WARREN, ROWE AND GILLEN, APRIL 21, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON HEALTH, APRIL 21, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the month of October 2025 as "Breast Cancer Awareness Month" and October 17, 2025, as "National Mammography Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, October has been long recognized across America as "Breast Cancer Awareness Month" in an effort to educate everyone about cancer and raise public awareness about the importance of early detection; andWHEREAS, Breast cancer is one of the most prevalent cancers worldwide and the most frequently diagnosed cancer among women in the United States after skin cancer; andWHEREAS, Breast cancer is also one of the most common cancer types among adolescent and young women; andWHEREAS, Breast cancer accounts for 30% of all diagnosed cancer cases in American adolescent and young adult women; andWHEREAS, One in eight women in the United States will face a breast cancer diagnosis during her lifetime; andWHEREAS, Approximately 140,000 women in this Commonwealth are 1234567891011121314151617 currently living with breast cancer; andWHEREAS, More than 13,000 women in this Commonwealth receive a breast cancer diagnosis each year; andWHEREAS, Breast cancer occurs in every country in the world and affects both men and women, although men account for less than 1% of breast cancer cases; andWHEREAS, In 2025, an estimated 316,950 women and 2,800 men will be diagnosed with invasive breast cancer; andWHEREAS, When detected in its earliest, localized stages, the five-year relative survival rate for breast cancer is an impressive 99%; andWHEREAS, There are currently more than 4 million breast cancer survivors across the United States; andWHEREAS, Clinical breast examinations, regular mammograms and breast self-examinations remain critical tools for detecting breast cancer; andWHEREAS, The use of mammography in the United States has been credited with doubling the detection of early stage breast cancer; andWHEREAS, Efforts to promote increased mammogram screening have resulted in the third Friday in October being observed annually as "National Mammography Day;" andWHEREAS, Significant advances in early detection and treatment methods have dramatically improved breast cancer survival rates in recent years; andWHEREAS, Regular mammography screening cuts the risk of dying from breast cancer nearly in half; andWHEREAS, Even with impressive progress, many women do not utilize mammograms or other recommended testing methods at regular intervals before physical symptoms can be seen or felt; andWHEREAS, "Breast Cancer Awareness Month" sheds light on preventative treatments and measures women can utilize to fight breast cancer before it occurs or detect breast cancer in its early stages; andWHEREAS, Thousands of professionals and volunteers fight against breast cancer and tirelessly work to raise funds, increase public awareness and provide support to those affected by breast cancer; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the month of October 2025 as "Breast Cancer Awareness Month" and October 17, 2025, as "National Mammography Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives encourage Pennsylvanians to use this month as an opportunity to educate themselves about breast cancer and take proactive steps to reduce their risks and get appropriate screenings;
