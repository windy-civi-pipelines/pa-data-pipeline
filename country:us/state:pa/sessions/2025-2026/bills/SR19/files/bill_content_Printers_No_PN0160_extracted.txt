2025-2026 Regular Session SR 19 PN 0160 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 160 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIASENATE RESOLUTION No.19 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY VOGEL, SCHWANK, LANGERHOLC, BROOKS, CULVER, PHILLIPS-HILL, COMITTA, KANE, FONTANA, MARTIN, J. WARD, DUSH, LAUGHLIN AND COSTA, JANUARY 31, 2025 REFERRED TO RULES AND EXECUTIVE NOMINATIONS, JANUARY 31, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the week of February 15 through 22, 2025, as "National FFA Week" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Future Farmers of America, now the National FFA Organization (FFA), has grown from its agricultural beginnings into a broad-spectrum leadership incubator; andWHEREAS, The FFA creed expresses belief in the future of agriculture with the following portion of its creed: "with a faith born not of words but of deeds"; andWHEREAS, In accordance with the FFA motto, members serve their chapter, community and country by cultivating friendships and planting new ideas and seeds of hope wherever they go:"Learning to Do, Doing to Learn, Earning to Live, Living to Serve";andWHEREAS, FFA in the 21st century welcomes young women and young men with diverse backgrounds and FFA membership has reached an all-time record high; and1234567891011121314151617 WHEREAS, More than 1 million FFA members from 50 states, the United States Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico are learning to be successful leaders in agriculture, agribusiness, agriscience and other fields; andWHEREAS, Many FFA members live far from working farms but have an interest in related disciplines, including food science, turf management and horticulture; andWHEREAS, FFA members who live in rural farm areas now represent 30% of the national organization; andWHEREAS, Nearly 16,000 students enrolled in agricultural education classes across this Commonwealth continue to wear the traditional blue corduroy jacket which distinguishes FFA members; andWHEREAS, Pennsylvania FFA is led by seven State officers who defer college and career for one year to advocate for agriculture, agricultural education and FFA; andWHEREAS, Chloe Bomgardner of Lebanon County presides as the State FFA President; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the Senate recognize the week of February 15 through 22, 2025, as "National FFA Week" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Senate congratulate the members, advisers and parents involved with this exemplary youth organization.20250SR0019PN0160 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223
