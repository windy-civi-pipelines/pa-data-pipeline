HARRIS, FRANKEL, KHAN, HILL-EVANS, WAXMAN, SANCHEZ, T. DAVIS, McNEILL, GIRAL, FREEMAN, PROBST, SCHLOSSBERG, HANBIDGE, PIELLI, DONAHUE, HOHENSTEIN, NEILSON, RIVERA, JAMES, DOUGHERTY, D. WILLIAMS, RABB, STEELE, BOROWSKI, SAPPEY, INGLIS, CONKLIN, GUZMAN, MAYES, O'MARA, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, CIRESI, SCHWEYER AND WARREN, JUNE 12, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON INSURANCE, JUNE 16, 2025 AN ACTAmending the act of November 22, 2005 (P.L.407, No.74), entitled "An act establishing a program for breast and cervical cancer screening services for certain eligible women; and providing for the powers and duties of the Department of Health," further providing for short title, for definitions and for Pennsylvania Breast and Cervical Cancer Early Screening Program established.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. Section 1 of the act of November 22, 2005 (P.L.407, No.74), known as the Pennsylvania Breast and Cervical Cancer Early Screening Act, is amended to read:Section 1. Short title.This act shall be known and may be cited as the Pennsylvania Breast, Colorectal and Cervical Cancer Early Screening Act.Section 2. The definition of "program" in section 2 of the act is amended and the section is amended by adding a definition to read:123456789101112131415161718 Section 2. Definitions.The following words and phrases when used in this act shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:* * *"Colorectal cancer screening." Any of the following procedures that are furnished to an individual for the purpose of early detection of colorectal cancer:(1) Screening fecal-occult blood or fecal immunochemical test.(2) Screening flexible sigmoidoscopy.(3) Screening colonoscopy.(4) Screening barium enema.(5) CT colonography.(6) Multi-target stool DNA test.(7) Screening tests consistent with approved medical standards and practices for the early detection of colorectal cancer.* * *"Program." The Pennsylvania Breast, Colorectal and Cervical Cancer Early Screening Program established in section 3.* * *Section 3. Section 3 of the act is amended to read:Section 3. Pennsylvania Breast, Colorectal and Cervical Cancer Early Screening Program established.(a) Program established.--There is hereby established within the Department of Health a program to be known as the Pennsylvania Breast, Colorectal and Cervical Cancer Early Screening Program, which shall provide for the availability of [annual] breast cancer screening services, colorectal cancer 20250HB1602PN1918 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 screening services and cervical cancer screening services [for low-income underinsured and uninsured women who are 40 to 49 years of age.] at intervals recommended by clinical practice guidelines based on age and sex.(b) Screening services.--The program shall be administered by the department for [women] individuals who are eligible under subsection (c) and shall be targeted towards [women] residents of this Commonwealth who are at high risk for breast, colorectal and cervical cancer.(c) Eligibility.--To be eligible for the program, an individual must meet all of the following requirements:(1) Be [a woman who is 40 to 49 years of age.] any of the following:(i) A woman who is 40 to 49 years of age for breast cancer and cervical cancer screenings.(ii) For colorectal cancer screenings, an individual who is:(A) 45 years of age or older, or 40 to 45 years of age with a family history of colon cancer; or(B) within 10 years of the age of a first-degree family relative previously diagnosed with colon cancer.(2) Have a family income that does not exceed 250% of the Federal poverty level.(3) Have no health insurance coverage for screening mammograms, clinical breast examinations, Pap [tests and pelvic examinations for women.], HPV or co-tests, pelvic examinations or colorectal cancer screenings.Section 4. This act shall take effect in 60 days.20250HB1602PN1918 - 3 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223242526272829
