AN ACT
Amending Title 34 (Game) of the Pennsylvania Consolidated
Statutes, in Pennsylvania Game Commission, further providing
for junior hunter projects; and, in game or wildlife
protection, further providing for regulations.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. Section 327(a) of Title 34 of the Pennsylvania
Consolidated Statutes is amended and the section is amended by
adding a subsection to read:
§ 327. Junior hunter projects.
(a) General rule.--It shall be the duty of the commission to
initiate, implement and administer two or more junior license
hunter projects and a mentored hunter season for the purpose of
increasing and sustaining interest in hunting among young
persons and mentored adults of this Commonwealth. Except for the
requirement to actually hold a junior hunting license under
section 2705(2) or (9) (relating to classes of licenses) or a
license under section 2701(c) (relating to license requirements)
and as may otherwise be modified by commission regulations, all

participants in any junior hunter project or mentored hunter

(c) Mentoring season.--
(1) The commission shall establish a seven-day mentoring
season immediately following the conclusion of the regular
firearms deer season.
(2) During the mentoring season, an individual holding a
license issued under section 2701(c) or 2705(2) or (9) may
hunt deer and small game species as designated by the
commission, provided the individual is accompanied by a
mentor.
Section 2. Section 2102 of Title 34 is amended by adding a
subsection to read:
§ 2102. Regulations.
* * *
(b.1) Regular antlered deer hunting season.--Notwithstanding
any other provision of law or regulation to the contrary, the
regular antlered deer hunting season shall open on:
(1) The first Monday following Thanksgiving in districts
1, 3, 5 and 7 as identified in section 301(b) (relating to
organization of commission).
(2) The first Saturday before Thanksgiving in districts
2, 4, 6, 8 and 9 as identified in section 301(b).
* * *
Section 3. This act shall take effect in 60 days.
