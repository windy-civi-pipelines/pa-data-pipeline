A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
Establishing the Pennsylvania State Song Commission to study the
history of the State song of the Commonwealth, solicit
submissions for a new State song and recommend changes to the
State song.
WHEREAS, During the 1987-1988 legislative session, the chair
of the House State Government Committee led a committee tasked
with recommending a State song for the Commonwealth; and
WHEREAS, The committee was composed of representatives from
the Pennsylvania Music Educators Association, the Pennsylvania
Council on the Arts, professional musicians and members of the
public; and
WHEREAS, After receiving hundreds of song submissions from
the public, the committee selected the song "Pennsylvania" by
Eddie Khoury and Ronnie Bonner; and
WHEREAS, The act of November 29, 1990 (P.L.598, No.150),
referred to as the Official State Song Act, adopted the song
"Pennsylvania" as the official State song of the Commonwealth;

WHEREAS, It is in the interest of this Commonwealth to adopt
a State song that represents the culture, history, spirit and
people of Pennsylvania; therefore be it
RESOLVED (the Senate concurring), That the General Assembly
establish the Pennsylvania State Song Commission to study the
history of the State song of the Commonwealth, solicit
submissions for a new State song and recommend changes to the
State song, which shall not be binding unless enacted by a
subsequent act of the General Assembly; and be it further
RESOLVED, That the commission consist of the following
members:
(1) one member appointed by the Majority Leader of the
Senate;
(2) one member appointed by the Minority Leader of the
Senate;
(3) one member appointed by the Majority Leader of the
House of Representatives;
(4) one member appointed by the Minority Leader of the
House of Representatives;
(5) one representative from the Pennsylvania Music
Educators Association;
(6) one representative from the Pennsylvania Council on
the Arts;
(7) one representative from the American Federation of
Musicians who, at the time of the formation of the
commission, resides in this Commonwealth; and
(8) two members of the public appointed by the Governor;
and be it further
RESOLVED, That the commission hold its first meeting within
60 days of the adoption of this resolution and meet no less than
