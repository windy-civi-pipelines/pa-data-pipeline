2025-2026 Regular Session HR 25 PN 0280 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 280 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.25 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY CIRESI, GIRAL, HADDOCK, SANCHEZ, GUENST, DIAMOND, HILL-EVANS, KHAN, NEILSON, FLEMING, VENKAT, PASHINSKI, WAXMAN, SOLOMON, CERRATO AND GREEN, JANUARY 27, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON STATE GOVERNMENT, JANUARY 27, 2025 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTIONEstablishing the Pennsylvania State Song Commission to study the history of the State song of the Commonwealth, solicit submissions for a new State song and recommend changes to the State song.WHEREAS, During the 1987-1988 legislative session, the chair of the House State Government Committee led a committee tasked with recommending a State song for the Commonwealth; andWHEREAS, The committee was composed of representatives from the Pennsylvania Music Educators Association, the Pennsylvania Council on the Arts, professional musicians and members of the public; andWHEREAS, After receiving hundreds of song submissions from the public, the committee selected the song "Pennsylvania" by Eddie Khoury and Ronnie Bonner; andWHEREAS, The act of November 29, 1990 (P.L.598, No.150), referred to as the Official State Song Act, adopted the song "Pennsylvania" as the official State song of the Commonwealth; and123456789101112131415161718 WHEREAS, It is in the interest of this Commonwealth to adopt a State song that represents the culture, history, spirit and people of Pennsylvania; therefore be itRESOLVED (the Senate concurring), That the General Assembly establish the Pennsylvania State Song Commission to study the history of the State song of the Commonwealth, solicit submissions for a new State song and recommend changes to the State song, which shall not be binding unless enacted by a subsequent act of the General Assembly; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission consist of the following members:(1) one member appointed by the Majority Leader of the Senate;(2) one member appointed by the Minority Leader of the Senate;(3) one member appointed by the Majority Leader of the House of Representatives;(4) one member appointed by the Minority Leader of the House of Representatives;(5) one representative from the Pennsylvania Music Educators Association;(6) one representative from the Pennsylvania Council on the Arts;(7) one representative from the American Federation of Musicians who, at the time of the formation of the commission, resides in this Commonwealth; and(8) two members of the public appointed by the Governor;and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission hold its first meeting within 60 days of the adoption of this resolution and meet no less than 20250HR0025PN0280 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 quarterly thereafter; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That at its first meeting, the commission elect one member to serve as chair of the commission; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission has the authority to add additional members to the commission and create subgroups within the commission as it sees fit; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission seek comments and submissions from the public on recommendations for a new State song; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission choose a song, or songs, to recommend to the General Assembly to become the new State song; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the members of the commission serve without compensation but be reimbursed for all necessary and reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of their duties; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That reasonable expenses incurred by the commission be equally divided between the Senate and the House of Representatives; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the approval of the President pro tempore of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives be required to preauthorize all proposed actions of the commission that will entail the expenditure of State funds and approve all expenditures, including expense reimbursement requests of commission members, prior to payment; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That nothing in this concurrent resolution preclude the commission from consulting or seeking the expertise of any individual, nonprofit entity, Commonwealth agency or office or legislative committee of the General Assembly to aid with the selection of a new State song; and be it further20250HR0025PN0280 - 3 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 RESOLVED, That the commission issue a final report with its findings and recommendations to the General Assembly within 12 months of the adoption of this resolution.20250HR0025PN0280 - 4 - 123
