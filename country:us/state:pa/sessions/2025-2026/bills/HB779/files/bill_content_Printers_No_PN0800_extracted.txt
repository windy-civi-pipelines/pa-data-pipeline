2025-2026 Regular Session HB 779 PN 0800 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 800 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE BILL No.779 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY SHUSTERMAN, HARKINS, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, INGLIS, PROBST, HILL-EVANS, CERRATO, HANBIDGE, KAZEEM, BOROWSKI, PIELLI, KHAN, SANCHEZ, DONAHUE, SCHLOSSBERG, D. WILLIAMS, CIRESI, GREEN, DEASY, MAYES, FLEMING, RIVERA, HOHENSTEIN, KENYATTA, GIRAL, MADDEN, GUENST AND ISAACSON, MARCH 3, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION, MARCH 3, 2025 AN ACTAmending the act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), entitled "An act relating to the public school system, including certain provisions applicable as well to private and parochial schools; amending, revising, consolidating and changing the laws relating thereto," in professional employees, providing for Head Start Teacher Salary Supplement Pilot Program.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. The act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), known as the Public School Code of 1949, is amended by adding a section to read:Section 1156. Head Start Teacher Salary Supplement Pilot Program.--(a) The Head Start Teacher Salary Supplement Pilot Program is established within the department to provide a supplement to bring Head Start teachers' salaries in line with elementary school teachers' salaries for comparable skills.(b) The pilot program shall operate for three school years beginning with the first school year after the effective date of 123456789101112131415161718 this subsection in accordance with the following:(1) No later than ninety (90) days after the effective date of this paragraph, the department shall establish guidelines and procedures for the implementation of this section.(2) The department shall select school districts and Head Start programs using the following criteria:(i) The school district shall have a poverty rate of at least twenty per centum (20%).(ii) A Head Start program not operated by a school district must be in a census tract with a median poverty level of eighty per centum (80%) or less of the Statewide median.(3) The department shall compare the salary and qualifications of the individual teachers in both the Head Start program and the teachers for the kindergarten, first grade, second grade, third grade and fourth grade classes at the public school that serves the district in which the Head Start program is based.(4) The department shall use a formula to calculate what the teachers' salaries would be if they were teaching in the district with the experience and credentials that the teacher currently holds.(5) The department shall provide a subsidy to the Head Start program to increase the teacher salaries to where the salary would be if the teachers were teaching in the public school district.(6) The department shall collect data and issue a report one year after the implementation of the pilot program in accordance with the following:(i) The department shall submit a report to the General Assembly detailing the pilot program, the implementation and any 20250HB0779PN0800 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 success or failure of the pilot program.(ii) The department shall provide recommendations for the General Assembly for improvements and revisions to the pilot program.(7) The department shall apply for Federal, State, private or other non-State funds.(8) The department shall ensure that all funds disbursed are being used solely for increasing teacher salaries. Misused funds must be recovered by the department.(c) A Head Start program selected for inclusion in the pilot program shall be responsible for the following:(1) Providing all necessary salary and employe information to the department.(2) Providing proof that any funds received are solely going to increasing teacher salaries and not toward lowering the cost of employment for the Head Start program.(d) As used in this section, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings given to them in this subsection unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:"Department" shall mean the Department of Education of the Commonwealth."Pilot program" shall mean the Head Start Teacher Salary Supplement Pilot Program established in subsection (a).Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.20250HB0779PN0800 - 3 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324
