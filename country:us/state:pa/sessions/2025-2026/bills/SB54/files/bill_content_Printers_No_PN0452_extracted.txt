Scope of subchapter.This subchapter relates to the provision of compensation for a wrongful conviction.§ 8582. Eligibility to seek compensation for wrongful conviction.(a) Cause of action authorized.--An action may be brought under this subchapter to seek compensation for the wrongful conviction of an individual. The claim may be brought by:(1) An individual who was convicted of a crime for which the individual was subsequently incarcerated or subject to involuntary treatment, has served all or any part of the sentence, alleges prima facie evidence of actual innocence of the crime or crimes and at least one of the following applies:(i) The conviction was dismissed, overturned, reversed or vacated on direct or collateral review on the basis of actual innocence and the charge was not refiled.(ii) The conviction was dismissed, overturned, reversed or vacated on direct or collateral review on the basis of actual innocence and the individual is acquitted after retrial.20250SB0054PN0452 - 3 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 (iii) The conviction was overturned, reversed or vacated on direct or collateral review on the basis of actual innocence and the individual subsequently entered an Alford plea or plea of no contest when otherwise eligible to seek retrial.(iv) A full pardon has been issued by the Governor on the basis of actual innocence.(2) The heirs of an individual meeting the criteria under paragraph (1), if the individual is deceased, notwithstanding if the heirs are residents of this Commonwealth.(b) Ineligibility for compensation.--A claimant may not seek compensation under this subchapter if any of the following apply:(1) The individual was an accomplice, coconspirator, solicitor or accessory in the commission of the crime for which the individual was originally convicted and meaningfully participated in the crime.(2) The individual was also serving a sentence for another crime for which the individual was lawfully incarcerated for the entirety of the time otherwise eligible for compensation.(3) The individual committed perjury or witness intimidation or fabricated evidence at the original trial. A proceeding before Commonwealth Court shall be governed by rules established by Commonwealth Court, which shall emphasize informality to the greatest extent possible. Compensation.(a) Granting of petition.--If Commonwealth Court grants a petition and enters an order in favor of the petitioner:(1) The court shall order the following:(i) Statutory noneconomic damages, as adjusted by subsection (c), of $100,000 for each year of imprisonment or involuntary treatment while awaiting a sentence of death.(ii) Statutory noneconomic damages, as adjusted by subsection (c), of $75,000 for each year of imprisonment or involuntary treatment for any other sentence.(iii) Statutory noneconomic damages, as adjusted by subsection (c), of $50,000 for each year spent on parole or probation.(2) The court may order the following if requested by the petitioner:(i) Reimbursement of unreimbursed costs, fines, fees or surcharges imposed on the individual as a result of 20250SB0054PN0452 - 8 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 the conviction which were paid by or on behalf of the individual.(ii) Reimbursement of unreimbursed restitution paid by the individual because of the conviction.(iii) Compensation for reasonable reintegrative services and mental and physical health care costs incurred by the individual for the period between the individual's release from incarceration or involuntary treatment and the date of the petitioner's award.(iv) Separation services and programs available to any other individual leaving incarceration.(v) Reasonable attorney fees for obtaining the dismissal, overturning, reversal or vacation of the underlying conviction, calculated at 10% of the damage awarded plus expenses. Exclusive of expenses, the fees may not exceed $75,000, as adjusted annually to account for inflation from the effective date of this section, unless the court approves an additional amount for good cause. The fees may not be deducted from the compensation awarded to the claimant nor may counsel receive additional fees from the client for this matter.(vi) Reasonable attorney fees and expenses for obtaining compensation under this section. The fees may not be deducted from the compensation awarded to the claimant, nor may counsel receive additional fees from the client for this matter.(vii) Child support payments owed by the individual that became due and interest on child support arrearages that accrued during the time the individual was wrongfully incarcerated but were not paid.
