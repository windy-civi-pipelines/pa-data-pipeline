2025-2026 Regular Session HR 346 PN 2466 Bill Text (HTM) PRINTER'S NO. 2466 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.346 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY ROWE, ANDERSON, BARGER, D'ORSIE, T. JONES, LEADBETER, GREINER, STAATS, WALSH, HAMM, ROAE, WATRO, ROSSI, KAUFFMAN, FINK AND KUZMA, OCTOBER 14, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON STATE GOVERNMENT, OCTOBER 16, 2025 A RESOLUTIONUrging the Department of State to comply with the lawful requests for information from the United States Department of Justice and immediately fulfill its obligations to adequately steward the election system of this Commonwealth by fully cooperating with Federal authorities in their efforts to prevent and investigate the registration of noncitizens to vote.WHEREAS, On June 23, August 4 and August 14, 2025, the United States Attorney General issued three letters requesting voter registration information from the Department of State to ascertain compliance with Federal laws, including the Help America Vote Act of 2002 (Public Law 107-252, 52 U.S.C. ยง 21081 et seq.) and the National Voter Registration Act of 1993 (Public Law 103-31, 52 U.S.C. ยง 20501 et seq.); andWHEREAS, On July 23, August 18 and August 21, 2025, the Department of State replied with only incomplete information, not to the satisfaction of the requests made by the United States Attorney General; andWHEREAS, On September 25, 2025, the United States Department of Justice filed suit against the Commonwealth and the Secretary 1234567891011121314151617181920 of the Commonwealth to compel the production of the requested information; andWHEREAS, The information requested by the United States Attorney General would provide the United States Department of Justice with the data required to adequately enforce Federal law regarding election integrity and voter registration administration; andWHEREAS, The refusal of the Department of State to timely produce the requested information will waste Pennsylvania tax dollars in litigation that the Commonwealth is likely to lose; andWHEREAS, The Department of State should properly share in the goals of the United States Department of Justice in fully utilizing all available information to investigate, prosecute and prevent election fraud and the registration of noncitizens to vote; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives urge the Department of State to comply with the lawful requests for information from the United States Department of Justice and immediately fulfill its obligations to adequately steward the election system of this Commonwealth by fully cooperating with Federal authorities in their efforts to prevent and investigate the registration of noncitizens to vote; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives call on the Governor to seek the fullest extent of collaboration between all of the executive agencies under his administration and the Federal Government as it seeks to duly enforce the immigration laws of this nation.20250HR0346PN2466 - 2 - 12345678910111213141516171819202122232425262728
