AN ACT
Amending the act of December 17, 1968 (P.L.1224, No.387),
entitled "An act prohibiting unfair methods of competition
and unfair or deceptive acts or practices in the conduct of
any trade or commerce, giving the Attorney General and
District Attorneys certain powers and duties and providing
penalties," further providing for definitions.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. Section 2(4) of the act of December 17, 1968
(P.L.1224, No.387), known as the Unfair Trade Practices and
Consumer Protection Law, is amended by adding a subclause to
read:
Section 2. Definitions.--As used in this act.
* * *
(4) "Unfair methods of competition" and "unfair or deceptive
acts or practices" mean any one or more of the following:
* * *
(xx.1) Using a form or other document related to a consumer
transaction that contains an automatic renewal provision or a

provision creating any other form of negative option plan. For
purposes of this subclause, the following shall apply:
(A) The term "negative option plan" means a transaction that
involves a seller and a consumer and includes the sending of
goods or performance of services not actually ordered or
requested by the consumer or that the consumer must opt out from
ordering or receiving, unless:
(I) The provision regarding the transaction is specified in
a clear and conspicuous manner, is in at least twelve-point
type, is boldfaced and includes all of the following:
(a) The specific procedure by which the consumer may cancel
at the end of the initial term and the terms of the automatic
renewal.
(b) An acknowledgment that includes the automatic renewal
offer terms and information regarding how to cancel in a manner
that is capable of being retained by the consumer.
(c) A guarantee that the seller will notify the consumer
before the automatic renewal occurs:
(i) Not more than fifteen days before a renewal period of
less than three months.
(ii) Not more than thirty days before a renewal period of
three months or more.
(iii) Using means of communication chosen by the consumer.
(II) The renewal period does not exceed twelve months.
(III) If the offer includes a free, discounted or otherwise
different introductory trial, all of the following apply:
(a) The seller discloses in the acknowledgment required
under subunit (I)(b) how to cancel and allow the consumer to
cancel before the consumer pays the full amount for the goods or
services.

action.
