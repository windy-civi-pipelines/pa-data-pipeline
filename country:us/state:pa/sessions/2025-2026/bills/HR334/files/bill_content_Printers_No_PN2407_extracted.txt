2025-2026 Regular Session HR 334 PN 2407 Bill Text (HTM) PRINTER'S NO. 2407 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.334 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY M. BROWN, BERNSTINE, WENTLING AND BONNER, OCTOBER 6, 2025 TO UNCONTESTED CALENDAR UNDER RULE 35 OCTOBER 6, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRemembering the distinguished life and service of the Honorable Ralph D. Pratt, former six-term member of the House of Representatives, who died August 18, 2025, in New Castle, Lawrence County, at 85 years of age.WHEREAS, The House of Representatives joins in commending Ralph D. Pratt's talents as a research chemist and chemical engineer, including his work on behalf of the Gemini space program; andWHEREAS, The House of Representatives honors Mr. Pratt's contributions to State government as an assistant attorney general, labor relations coordinator and legislator; andWHEREAS, The House of Representatives notes Mr. Pratt's dedication to our legal and judicial systems as an attorney and judge; andWHEREAS, Elected to the House of Representatives from the 10th District, Mr. Pratt represented Lawrence and Mercer Counties from 1975 through 1982 and Beaver, Lawrence and Mercer Counties from 1983 through January 6, 1986; andWHEREAS, Mr. Pratt was appointed to the Joint Legislative Air 12345678910111213141516171819 and Water Pollution Control and Conservation Committee in the early 1980s; andWHEREAS, Elected in 1985 to a newly created third seat on the Lawrence County Court of Common Pleas, Mr. Pratt's focus on the bench extended from family-related cases to civil cases and trials; andWHEREAS, From 2000 through June 2004, Mr. Pratt managed and supported the county court and minor court systems and related agencies as president judge; andWHEREAS, Mr. Pratt heard cases as needed as a senior judge; andWHEREAS, Mr. Pratt attended New Castle schools and Lincoln High School in Ellwood City; andWHEREAS, Mr. Pratt held a bachelor of science degree in chemistry from Baldwin-Wallace College and a juris doctor degree from the University of Pittsburgh School of Law; andWHEREAS, Mr. Pratt earned master's degrees in health law from the University of Pittsburgh Graduate School of Public Health and judicial studies from the University of Nevada; andWHEREAS, Mr. Pratt's early study of statutory law and legal sanctions relating to physician assistants was published in 1972; andWHEREAS, The House of Representatives expresses condolences to Mr. Pratt's family, with grateful recognition of his significant work as a public servant; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives remember the distinguished life and service of the Honorable Ralph D. Pratt, former six-term member of the House of Representatives, who died August 18, 2025, in New Castle, Lawrence County, at 85 years of age; and be it further20250HR0334PN2407 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 RESOLVED, That a copy of this resolution be transmitted to Mrs. Susan Pratt.20250HR0334PN2407 - 3 - 12
