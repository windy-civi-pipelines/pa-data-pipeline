MACKENZIE, VENKAT, WATRO, STAATS AND PUGH, SEPTEMBER 24, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON LABOR AND INDUSTRY, SEPTEMBER 25, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating October 3, 2025, as "Manufacturing Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Manufacturing is a cornerstone of this Commonwealth's economy, generating more than $112 billion each year; andWHEREAS, Manufacturing is a major driver of this Commonwealth's economy, accounting for approximately 13% of gross State product; andWHEREAS, Manufacturers employ more than 562,000 hardworking Pennsylvanians in facilities across this Commonwealth; andWHEREAS, Additional Pennsylvania jobs are directly sustained through supply chains, distribution networks, warehousing and logistics tied to manufacturing; andWHEREAS, Manufacturing careers provide above-average wages and benefits; andWHEREAS, Manufacturing is more than making products and plays a vital role in shaping the future by relying on creators and 1234567891011121314151617 innovators to drive progress and competitiveness; andWHEREAS, Modern manufacturing relies heavily on advanced technology, including robotics and additive manufacturing; andWHEREAS, Many manufacturing careers are accessible through on-the-job training, apprenticeships, certificates or associate degrees, creating clear pathways for students and young adults; andWHEREAS, Greater awareness will help attract students and young adults to opportunities in manufacturing for professional growth and family-sustaining work; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives designate October 3, 2025, as "Manufacturing Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives, in conjunction with national and State efforts, recognize the economic contributions of manufacturing in this Commonwealth and its role in building the workforce of tomorrow.20250HR0325PN2339 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617
