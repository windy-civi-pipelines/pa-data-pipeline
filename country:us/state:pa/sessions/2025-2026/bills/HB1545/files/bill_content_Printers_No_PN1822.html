§ 1379. Suspension of registration upon [sixth] unpaid parking
[violation in cities of the first class] violations.
(a) Suspension of registration.--The department shall
suspend the registration of a vehicle upon the notification from
the parking authority or a parking enforcement office that the
owner or registrant of the vehicle has failed to respond, failed
to pay or defaulted in:
(1) the payment of six or more tickets or citations
issued for parking violations [in cities of the first class.]
by a parking authority or parking enforcement office; or
(2) the payment of more than $500 in cumulative fines or
civil penalties issued for parking violations by the parking
authority or a parking enforcement office.
(b) Notice to the department.--No sooner than 30 days after
mailing the notice provided under subsection (b.1), the parking
authority or parking enforcement office shall notify the
department electronically in a format prescribed by the
department whenever an owner or registrant fails to respond,
fails to pay or defaults in payment of [six or more tickets or
citations issued for] parking violations as provided for under
subsection (a). When a notice has been provided under this
subsection and all of the tickets, civil penalties and citations
are subsequently paid, dismissed, reversed on appeal or
canceled, the parking authority or parking enforcement office
shall notify the department electronically in a format
prescribed by the department of the disposition of the tickets
or civil penalties and shall provide the owner or registrant
with a release from the suspension.

(b.1) Notice by the parking authority or parking enforcement
office.--Prior to notifying the department under subsection (b),
the parking authority or parking enforcement office shall
provide the owner or registrant written notice by first class
mail of its intent to seek suspension of the vehicle
registration pursuant to this section.
(c) Period of suspension.--A suspension under subsection (a)
shall continue until the department receives notice from the
parking authority or parking enforcement office that all of the
tickets, civil penalties and citations are paid, dismissed,
reversed on appeal or canceled or the defendant enters into an
agreement to make installment payments for the fines and
penalties imposed, provided that the suspension may be reimposed
by the department if the defendant fails to make regular
installment payments and pays the fee prescribed in section 1960
(relating to reinstatement of operating privilege or vehicle
registration).
(d) Additional suspension.--The department shall impose an
additional period of registration suspension if, subsequent to
the issuance of a suspension under subsection (a) but prior to
the restoration of the registration, the department is notified
by the parking authority or parking enforcement office that the
owner or registrant has failed to respond, failed to pay or
defaulted in the payment of an additional ticket, civil penalty
or citation issued for a parking violation.
(e) Three-year limitation.--No suspension may be imposed
based upon a parking violation more than three years after the
commission of the violation.

Definitions.--As used in this section, the following words and

unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Parking authority." A parking authority in a city of the
first class known as the Philadelphia Parking Authority.
"Parking enforcement office." A parking authority authorized
under 53 Pa.C.S. Ch. 55 (relating to parking authorities) that
is not in a city of the first class or a police department that
enforces parking violations and is not in a city of the first
class.
"Police department." The term shall have the same meaning as
defined under 53 Pa.C.S. § 2162 (relating to definitions).
