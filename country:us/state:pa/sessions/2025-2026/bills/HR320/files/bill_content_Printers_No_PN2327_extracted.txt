2025-2026 Regular Session HR 320 PN 2327 Bill Text (HTM) PRINTER'S NO. 2327 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.320 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY STAMBAUGH, ANDERSON, JAMES, PICKETT, GAYDOS, PROBST, ZIMMERMAN, BARGER AND GILLEN, SEPTEMBER 18, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON TRANSPORTATION, SEPTEMBER 22, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDirecting the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee to conduct a study and issue a report on the current status, management and implementation of modernization practices for the Department of Transportation.WHEREAS, Modernization of the Department of Transportation to exhibit efficient, reliable, cost-effective and improved methods of practice is essential for the economic well-being of all communities across this Commonwealth; andWHEREAS, The Department of Transportation is crucial in providing sufficient transportation needs throughout this Commonwealth and has the responsibility of ensuring that the effective and efficient modernization of the transportation system takes place to benefit residents across this Commonwealth; andWHEREAS, Investing in and improving modernization efforts for the transportation system across this Commonwealth can enhance mobility, stimulate economic growth, reduce traffic congestion, enhance environmental sustainability, improve overall quality of life and create cost-effective practices; and12345678910111213141516171819 WHEREAS, A comprehensive study specifically focusing on the modernization of the practices of the Department of Transportation will provide valuable insights into the constructive transportation needs and opportunities facing this Commonwealth, challenges that present themselves in modernization, contemporary investment decisions and policy and future investment decisions and policy; andWHEREAS, By conducting a study on the modernization of the practices of the Department of Transportation, the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee can gather data; evaluate existing Department of Transportation practices by identifying gaps, deficiencies and shortfalls; explore innovative solutions; and recommend strategies to modernize Department of Transportation practices; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives direct the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee to conduct a study and issue a report on the current status, management and implementation of modernization practices for the Department of Transportation; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the study:(1) Assess the existing means of procurement for the Department of Transportation.(2) Identify improvements in electronic paperless transactions and explore existing paper transactions to be offered by electronic paperless means.(3) Evaluate the existing Department of Transportation Engineering Districts and recommend changes or reforms to the structure of each district considering factors, including population change, county size, highway miles, accessibility, maintenance costs, material costs, employment and access to 20250HR0320PN2327 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 essential services.(4) Explore modernization efforts and cost-effective practices relating to road and bridge funding options.(5) Engage with local communities, stakeholders, leader, transportation experts and other relevant entities to gather input, feedback and insights throughout the study.(6) Develop a comprehensive set of recommendations and strategies to enhance modernization of the practices of the Department of Transportation, considering cost-effectiveness, long-term sustainability, accessibility and the stimulation of economic growth;and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee prepare a report of its findings and recommendations and submit the report to each member of the House of Representative no later than 12 months after the adoption of this resolution.20250HR0320PN2327 - 3 - 12345678910111213141516
