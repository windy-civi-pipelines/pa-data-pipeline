AN ACT
Amending Title 35 (Health and Safety) of the Pennsylvania
Consolidated Statutes, providing for mental health crisis
response; and imposing duties on the Department of Human
Services.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. Title 35 of the Pennsylvania Consolidated
Statutes is amended by adding a chapter to read:
CHAPTER 53A
MENTAL HEALTH CRISIS RESPONSE
Sec.
53A01. Definitions.
53A02. 988 public education campaign.
53A03. Evaluation and report.
53A01. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this chapter
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the

"911 system." As defined in section 5302 (relating to
definitions).
"988 crisis call center." A call center designated by the
Commonwealth to respond to Statewide or regional calls from the
988 suicide and crisis lifeline.
"988 suicide and crisis lifeline." The universal telephone
number within the United States designated for the purpose of
the national suicide prevention and mental health crisis hotline
system operating through the National Suicide Prevention
Lifeline program in accordance with 42 U.S.C. 290bb-36c
(relating to National Suicide Prevention Lifeline program).
"Campaign." The 988 public education campaign described
under section 53A02 (relating to 988 public education campaign).
"Department." The Department of Human Services of the
Commonwealth.
"Emergency responder." Any of the following:
(1) A current or former certified emergency medical
services provider or current or former member of an emergency
medical services agency, fire company or rescue company.
(2) A peace officer.
(3) A 911 dispatcher.
(4) A coroner or medical examiner who responds in an
official capacity to an emergency.
(5) A corrections officer.

effective date of this subsection, SUBJECT TO AVAILABLE FUNDING,
the department shall implement a campaign to increase public
understanding and engagement with the 988 suicide and crisis

53A03. Evaluation and report.
(a) Initial report.--Within 180 days of the effective date
of this subsection, the department shall submit a report to the
chairperson and minority chairperson of the Health and Human
Services Committee of the Senate and the chairperson and
minority chairperson of the Human Services Committee of the
House of Representatives. The department shall ensure that the
report, at a minimum, includes all of the following:
(1) A comprehensive review of the department's efforts
to raise awareness of the 988 suicide and crisis lifeline,
detailing activities, key metrics utilized and budget
allocations and expenditures.
(2) A performance summary, including call volume data
and the usage of the 988 suicide and crisis lifeline chat and
text functions, key findings, recommendations and any
preliminary or final reports generated from efforts to raise

(3) Information on 988 suicide and crisis lifeline

(4) Insights or recommendations from the consultations

(1) Updated statistics on 988 suicide and crisis
lifeline operations.
(2) Information on new campaigns or initiatives to raise
awareness of the 988 suicide and crisis lifeline, including
metrics, target audiences and performance evaluations.
(3) The impact of the new campaigns or initiatives under
paragraph (2) on the utilization of the 988 suicide and
crisis lifeline, including the in-State answer rate, call
volume and the number of Pennsylvanians receiving services or
referrals.
Section 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
