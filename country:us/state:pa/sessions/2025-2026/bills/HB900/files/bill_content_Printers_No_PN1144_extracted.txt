2025-2026 Regular Session HB 900 PN 1144 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRIOR PASSAGE - NONE PRINTER'S NO. 1144 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE BILL No.900 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY DIAMOND, HAMM, STAATS, SMITH, KENYATTA, KAUFFMAN, COOK, GILLEN, RADER, D'ORSIE, WARNER AND ZIMMERMAN, MARCH 26, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON FINANCE, MARCH 26, 2025 A JOINT RESOLUTIONProposing an amendment to the Constitution of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, prohibiting the taxation of real property.The General Assembly finds and declares as follows:(1) Section 1 of Article I of the Constitution of Pennsylvania declares that acquiring, possessing and protecting property are inherent rights of mankind.(2) These rights have been recognized since the Commonwealth's liberation from tyranny in 1776.(3) These rights are being abrogated by circumstances within the Commonwealth and its political subdivisions, causing residents to be deprived of their property for no reason other than an inability to pay taxes.(4) The highest duty of a government of free people is to protect such inherent rights.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby resolves as follows:Section 1. The following amendment to the Constitution of 1234567891011121314151617 Pennsylvania is proposed in accordance with Article XI:That Article VIII be amended by adding a section to read:ยง 18. Real property. Beginning July 1, 2030, all taxation, assessment and valuation of real property by the Commonwealth, or by any authority, district, jurisdiction or political subdivision within this Commonwealth, shall be prohibited.Section 2. The following procedure applies to the proposed constitutional amendment in this joint resolution:(1) Upon the first passage by the General Assembly of the amendment, the Secretary of the Commonwealth shall proceed immediately to comply with the advertising requirements of section 1 of Article XI of the Constitution of Pennsylvania.(2) Upon the second passage by the General Assembly of the amendment, the Secretary of the Commonwealth shall proceed immediately to comply with the advertising requirements of section 1 of Article XI of the Constitution of Pennsylvania.
