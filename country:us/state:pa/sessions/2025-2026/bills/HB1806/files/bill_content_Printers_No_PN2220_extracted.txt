Title 38 of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes is amended by adding a section to read:ยง 1307. 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion Day.(a) Legislative findings and declarations.--The General Assembly finds and declares as follows:(1) In November 1944, the United States War Department lifted the ban on African American women in the Womens' Army Corps, leading to the formation of the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion.(2) The 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion was a unique United States Army unit that had the distinction of being the only all African American all-female battalion to be deployed to the European Theater of Operations during 123456789101112131415161718 World War II.(3) Over 850 African American women, including 824 enlisted and 31 officers, served with the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion which was commissioned February 1945 to March 1946.(4) The specific mission of the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion during World War II was to sort and clear a multiyear backlog of mail for the American Army, Navy, Air Force, Red Cross and uniformed civilian specialists stationed in Europe, which represented 7 million individuals awaiting correspondence.(5) The 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion trained at Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia, and learned to:(i) Identify enemy aircraft, ships and weapons.(ii) Climb ropes, jump trenches and crawl under logs wearing gas masks.(iii) Board and evacuate ships.(iv) March long distances with rucksacks.(6) In February 1945, the first contingent of the 6888th embarked from Camp Shank, New York, to sail to Britain and their ship, the Ile de France, survived close encounters with the Nazi U-boats, including a V-1 rocket exploding near the dock causing the women to run and cover, and arrived safely in Glasgow, Scotland.(7) Despite enduring inhumane working conditions, including dark, unheated, rat-infested aircraft hangers with broken windows and air raids, the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion created a new mail tracking system, worked three separate eight-hour shifts, seven days per week to process an average of 65,000 parcels per shift, or 195,000 20250HB1806PN2220 - 2
