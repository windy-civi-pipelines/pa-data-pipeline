AN ACT
Amending the act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), entitled "An
act relating to the public school system, including certain
provisions applicable as well to private and parochial
schools; amending, revising, consolidating and changing the
laws relating thereto," establishing the Early Childhood
Educator Tuition Assistance Program and the Early Childhood
Educator Tuition Assistance Fund.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. The act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), known
as the Public School Code of 1949, is amended by adding an
article to read:
ARTICLE XIX-I
EARLY CHILDHOOD EDUCATOR TUITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM

Section 1902-I. Early Childhood Educator Tuition Assistance
Program.
(a) Establishment and purpose.--The Early Childhood Educator
Tuition Assistance Program is established within the agency to
provide grants in the form of tuition assistance to individuals
seeking an associate degree or certification in early childhood
education.
(b) Eligibility.--An applicant for a grant under the program
shall:
(1) Report a household income that is not more than 400%
of the Federal poverty level.
(2) Be enrolled or accepted to be enrolled in a minimum
of six credit hours per semester, or its equivalent, in a
credit-bearing or certification-granting early childhood
education program.

shall occur at the end of the academic term for which the
student receives the grant.

Section 1903-I. Early Childhood Educator Tuition Assistance
Fund.
(a) Establishment and purpose.--The Early Childhood Educator
Tuition Assistance Fund is established in the State Treasury to
make appropriations to the agency to carry out the provisions of
this article.
(b) Appropriations.--The fund shall consist of an annual
appropriation or transfer from the General Fund, as well as
money received from the Federal Government and any
appropriations by the General Assembly for the purposes of
funding the program.
(c) Requests.--The agency shall request funding for the
program in an amount equivalent to the obligations of the
program and shall not direct money from the fund for any other
purpose.
Section 1904-I. Reports.
(a) Publication and contents.--By September 1, 2025, and
each September 1 thereafter, the agency shall publish a report
that includes the following information regarding the operation
of the program for the immediately preceding fiscal year:
(1) Data on student enrollment, student retention rates
between academic terms and years and student wages,
including:
(i) Median wages prior to enrollment and one year
after completion of a credential or degree.
(ii) Wage rates of students who have not enrolled in
over a year and did not complete a credential or degree.
(2) A comparison of job demand in early childhood
