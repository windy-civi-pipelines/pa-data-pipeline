A RESOLUTION
Recognizing March 8, 2025, as "International Women's Day" in
Pennsylvania with respect for women's rich and diverse
accomplishments and aspirations.
WHEREAS, "International Women's Day" is observed globally on
March 8 of every year to recognize the social, economic,
cultural, political, workforce and leadership roles of women;
and
WHEREAS, The Commonwealth recognizes the importance of
supporting and empowering women leaders in all sectors and
levels of society, and of fostering a culture of diversity and
respect; and
WHEREAS, On "International Women's Day," thousands of events
are held throughout the world to honor women while focusing
attention on areas requiring further action; and
WHEREAS, These events are a reminder of the hard work,
sacrifices and joy evident in the notable progress women have
won through the years; and

WHEREAS, One of the most iconic symbols of "International
Women's Day" is the "Fearless Girl" statue which debuted on
"International Women's Day" seven years ago in downtown
Manhattan across from the "Charging Bull" statue; and
WHEREAS, A plaque originally placed below the "Fearless Girl"
statue read "Know the power of women in leadership. SHE makes a
difference"; and
WHEREAS, The "Fearless Girl" statue continues to inspire
women and girls around the world to be fearless and pursue their
dreams; and
WHEREAS, "International Women's Day" is an opportunity for
Pennsylvanians to highlight the numerous contributions of women
across this Commonwealth; and
WHEREAS, This Commonwealth has been home to many influential
and groundbreaking women from various fields and backgrounds,
including environmentalists, authors, scientists, seamstresses,
entertainers, civil rights activists and the like; and
WHEREAS, Women play a vital role in all spheres of
government, from local school boards to the Pennsylvania House
of Representatives, bringing their unique insight and
perspective; and
WHEREAS, "International Women's Day" is an ideal day to
celebrate the achievements of women across this Commonwealth and
specifically the record number of women serving in the
Pennsylvania House of Representatives and Senate, as well as the
first female Speaker of the House of Representatives and
President pro tempore of the Senate; and
WHEREAS, There are countless women across our Commonwealth
who have made a significant impact on society; and
WHEREAS, Women's leadership helps build stronger communities,
