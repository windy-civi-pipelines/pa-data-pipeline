MACKENZIE, MERSKI, VENKAT, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, GIRAL, KHAN, SAMUELSON, CONKLIN, DONAHUE, MALAGARI, D. WILLIAMS, CIRESI, SHUSTERMAN, DEASY, O'MARA, SANCHEZ, GREEN AND DALEY, MARCH 3, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON STATE GOVERNMENT, MARCH 3, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing March 8, 2025, as "International Women's Day" in Pennsylvania with respect for women's rich and diverse accomplishments and aspirations.WHEREAS, "International Women's Day" is observed globally on March 8 of every year to recognize the social, economic, cultural, political, workforce and leadership roles of women; andWHEREAS, The Commonwealth recognizes the importance of supporting and empowering women leaders in all sectors and levels of society, and of fostering a culture of diversity and respect; andWHEREAS, On "International Women's Day," thousands of events are held throughout the world to honor women while focusing attention on areas requiring further action; andWHEREAS, These events are a reminder of the hard work, sacrifices and joy evident in the notable progress women have won through the years; and1234567891011121314151617 WHEREAS, One of the most iconic symbols of "International Women's Day" is the "Fearless Girl" statue which debuted on "International Women's Day" seven years ago in downtown Manhattan across from the "Charging Bull" statue; andWHEREAS, A plaque originally placed below the "Fearless Girl" statue read "Know the power of women in leadership. SHE makes a difference"; andWHEREAS, The "Fearless Girl" statue continues to inspire women and girls around the world to be fearless and pursue their dreams; andWHEREAS, "International Women's Day" is an opportunity for Pennsylvanians to highlight the numerous contributions of women across this Commonwealth; andWHEREAS, This Commonwealth has been home to many influential and groundbreaking women from various fields and backgrounds, including environmentalists, authors, scientists, seamstresses, entertainers, civil rights activists and the like; andWHEREAS, Women play a vital role in all spheres of government, from local school boards to the Pennsylvania House of Representatives, bringing their unique insight and perspective; andWHEREAS, "International Women's Day" is an ideal day to celebrate the achievements of women across this Commonwealth and specifically the record number of women serving in the Pennsylvania House of Representatives and Senate, as well as the first female Speaker of the House of Representatives and President pro tempore of the Senate; andWHEREAS, There are countless women across our Commonwealth who have made a significant impact on society; andWHEREAS, Women's leadership helps build stronger communities, 20250HR0096PN0826 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 organizations and nations; andWHEREAS, It is important to acknowledge the value of women in leadership positions and encourage more women to pursue these roles; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize March 8, 2025, as "International Women's Day" in Pennsylvania with respect for women's rich and diverse accomplishments and aspirations; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives encourage residents of this Commonwealth to participate in the appropriate events and activities of "International Women's Day."20250HR0096PN0826 - 3 - 1234567891011
