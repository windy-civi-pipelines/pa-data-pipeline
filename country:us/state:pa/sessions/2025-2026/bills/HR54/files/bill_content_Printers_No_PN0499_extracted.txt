2025-2026 Regular Session HR 54 PN 0499 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 499 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.54 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY KAZEEM, VITALI, SANCHEZ, GIRAL, HILL-EVANS, HADDOCK, HOHENSTEIN, KHAN, CIRESI, FREEMAN, DELLOSO, KENYATTA, O'MARA, ZIMMERMAN AND GREEN, FEBRUARY 5, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON TOURISM, RECREATION AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT, FEBRUARY 5, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating the month of October 2025 as "William Penn Landing Site in Chester Month" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, William Penn, son of Admiral Sir William Penn, was born in London on October 14, 1644; andWHEREAS, William Penn shocked his contemporaries by converting to the beliefs of the Society of Friends, or Quakers, which was then a persecuted sect; andWHEREAS, William Penn used his wealth and status to benefit and protect his fellow believers; andWHEREAS, King Charles II owed William Penn £16,000, which was money his late father lent to the king; andWHEREAS, William Penn requested that the debt be settled with land in the New World to provide a safe haven for his fellow Quakers; andWHEREAS, William Penn requested land between Lord Baltimore's province of Maryland and the Duke of York's province of New York; and1234567891011121314151617 WHEREAS, With the Duke's support, Penn's petition was granted; andWHEREAS, King Charles II signed the Charter of Pennsylvania on March 4, 1681, and it was officially proclaimed on April 2; andWHEREAS, The colony was named in honor of William Penn's father; andWHEREAS, The colony included lands between the 39th and 42nd degrees of north latitude and from the Delaware River westward for five degrees of longitude; andWHEREAS, The Duke of York also deeded to Penn his claim to what is now the state of Delaware; andWHEREAS, William Penn set sail for Pennsylvania on a ship called the Welcome; andWHEREAS, After 57 days, the Welcome arrived in New Castle, Delaware; andWHEREAS, However, William Penn did not disembark until he docked in Chester, Pennsylvania, on what is believed to be October 29, 1682; andWHEREAS, William Penn marveled at the place and found the land good and the air serene; andWHEREAS, William Penn renamed the area, which was called Upland by Swedish settlers, Chester after the city in Cheshire County, England; andWHEREAS, Chester became Pennsylvania's first incorporated municipality and William Penn had grand visions for the area; andWHEREAS, William Penn summoned the settlement's largest landowner, James Sandelands, to negotiate a deal to establish Chester as the capital of Pennsylvania; and20250HR0054PN0499 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 WHEREAS, William Penn and James Sandelands were unable to reach a deal and William Penn looked elsewhere and eventually founded Philadelphia;
