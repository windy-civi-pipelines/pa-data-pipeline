2025-2026 Regular Session HR 161 PN 1217 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1217 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.161 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, VITALI, HILL-EVANS, KHAN, SCHLOSSBERG, SANCHEZ, GIRAL, HADDOCK, MERSKI, NEILSON, FREEMAN, VENKAT, BRENNAN, DALEY, HOHENSTEIN, KENYATTA, GREEN AND PASHINSKI, APRIL 1, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON TOURISM, RECREATION AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT, APRIL 1, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating the month of May 2025 as "Haitian Heritage Month" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Haiti is the third largest Caribbean country and the first Black Republic in the world; andWHEREAS, When Haitians gained independence, they renamed their country to its native Taino name of Ayiti, or "Land of Mountains"; andWHEREAS, From 1791 to 1804, Haitians led a revolution against French colonialists that ultimately resulted in their independence; andWHEREAS, Haiti was also the first nation to permanently ban slavery, largely contributing to Great Britain's decision to abolish the transatlantic slave trade; andWHEREAS, The Haitian flag was adopted on May 18, 1803, to symbolize the "pride, resilience, and spirit of the Haitian people in their fight for freedom and independence"; and12345678910111213141516 WHEREAS, Haiti has supported the independence of countries such as Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador and Greece; andWHEREAS, As a result of Haiti's support of liberation in other countries, many nations have paid homage to Haiti by using the Haitian flag as a model for their own flags; andWHEREAS, By forcing Napoleon Bonaparte to sell what is now the state of Louisiana, the Haitian Revolution led to the Louisiana Purchase and territorial expansion of the United States; andWHEREAS, The cultural influence of Haitians on United States society and culture has only grown since Haitians first started emigrating to the United States in 1790; andWHEREAS, As of 2023, there were approximately 1.15 million Haitian individuals residing in the United States, including those born in Haiti and of Haitian descent; andWHEREAS, "Haitian Heritage Month" is an extension of "Haitian Flag Day" on May 18, a day that encourages patriotism in Haiti and the diaspora; andWHEREAS, "Haitian Heritage Month" is recognized across the globe and serves as an opportunity to celebrate the culture, art, cuisine and traditions of Haiti and its people; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives designate the month of May 2025 as "Haitian Heritage Month" in Pennsylvania.20250HR0161PN1217 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324
