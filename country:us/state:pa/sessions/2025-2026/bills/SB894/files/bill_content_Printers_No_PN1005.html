AN ACT
Amending the act of June 2, 1915 (P.L.736, No.338), entitled "An
act defining the liability of an employer to pay damages for
injuries received by an employe in the course of employment;
establishing an elective schedule of compensation; providing
procedure for the determination of liability and compensation
thereunder; and prescribing penalties," in liability and
compensation, providing for registration of status as
independent contractor.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. The act of June 2, 1915 (P.L.736, No.338), known
as the Workers' Compensation Act, is amended by adding a section
to read:
Section 304.3. (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of
this act , a person may file a registration with the department
regarding the status of an individual who is classified for
Federal income tax purposes as an independent contractor of the
person.
(b) A registration under this section shall include the
following:
(1) A written waiver by the individual of all benefits under

(2) An affidavit by the individual specifying that the
individual understands and agrees that the individual:
(i) is not an employe of the person;
(ii) is not entitled to benefits under this act from the
person;
(iii) may purchase workers' compensation insurance under
this act or other disability insurance at the individual's own
expense; and
(iv) is not entitled to benefits from the Uninsured
Employers Guaranty Fund if the individual is injured while
performing services for the person.
(c) The waiver and affidavit under subsection (b) shall be
made upon forms prescribed by the department.
(d) (1) A person who registers an individual as an
independent contractor under this section:
(i) shall not be liable to the individual for benefits under
this act; and
(ii) shall not be required to provide insurance coverage or
self-insure under this act regarding the individual.
(2) An individual registered as an independent contractor
under this section shall not be eligible for benefits from the
Uninsured Employers Guaranty Fund if the person is injured while
performing services as an independent contractor pursuant to the
registration.
(e) A registration identifying an individual as an
independent contractor shall be valid for each future year,
except that the individual may request that the registration be
revoked at any time. The following apply:
(1) The request for revocation shall be on a form prescribed

an individual to complete the waiver and affidavit under
subsection (b) or refrain from filing a request for revocation

(1) Knowingly provide an incentive to the individual,

(2) Knowingly provide materially false information to the
individual.
(3) Knowingly withhold information from the individual in
order to deceive the individual.
(g) A person violating subsection (f) shall be subject to
the penalties for an intentional violation of section 305.

(1) "Independent contractor" includes an individual who

who is not classified as an employe of the person for Federal
income tax purposes.

Section 2. The addition of section 304.3 of the act shall
not be construed to alter, repeal or supersede any provision of

Section 3. This act shall take effect in 180 days.
