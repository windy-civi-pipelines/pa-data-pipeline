2025-2026 Regular Session HR 167 PN 1223 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1223 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.167 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY SAPPEY, WAXMAN, MADDEN, GUENST, HILL-EVANS, HANBIDGE, GIRAL, FREEMAN, STEELE, OTTEN, SANCHEZ, BOROWSKI AND NEILSON, APRIL 1, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON ENVIRONMENTAL AND NATURAL RESOURCE PROTECTION, APRIL 1, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing May 9 through 18, 2025, as "Go Public Gardens Days" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, "Go Public Gardens Days" is an ongoing initiative to drive the public to visit, value and volunteer at public gardens; andWHEREAS, Public gardens comprise botanical gardens, arboreta, cemeteries, zoological gardens, sculpture gardens, college and university campuses, historical homes, urban greening organizations, natural areas and Federal, State, county and city parks; andWHEREAS, "PA Gardens" represents a diverse coalition of Pennsylvania's public horticulture organizations and its vision is to create opportunities for every Pennsylvanian to engage with and benefit from organizations providing public horticulture experiences throughout their communities; andWHEREAS, Public gardens are essential drivers of workforce and economic development, supporting more than 2,000 full-time 1234567891011121314151617 employees in this Commonwealth, driving more than $500 million into this Commonwealth's economy and supporting $164 million in combined operating budgets; andWHEREAS, Public gardens in this Commonwealth welcome more than 4.2 million visitors annually, which is on par with professional sports league attendance; andWHEREAS, One third of these visitors are from out of town, producing millions in visitor spending throughout our communities; andWHEREAS, Public gardens are vital to feeding humanity and bring together diverse expertise, approaches and solutions that are essential for biodiversity in food and agriculture; andWHEREAS, Public gardens are involved in education and research and development efforts that bring together schools, government, industry and nonprofits to cultivate a healthy world through landscapes, gardens and plants; andWHEREAS, Public gardens prioritize open green space and conservation by preserving thousands of acres of natural resources through conservation, restored forests, improved watersheds and healthy private working lands; andWHEREAS, Public gardens have a positive impact on personal health and well-being; andWHEREAS, Studies have shown that connecting people to plants and the outdoors boosts well-being by providing many physical, emotional and social benefits; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the week of May 9 through 18, 2025, as "Go Public Gardens Days" in Pennsylvania.20250HR0167PN1223 - 2 - 12345678910111213141516171819202122232425262728
