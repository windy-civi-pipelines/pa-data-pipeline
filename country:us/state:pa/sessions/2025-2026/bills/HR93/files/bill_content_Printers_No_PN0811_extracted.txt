2025-2026 Regular Session HR 93 PN 0811 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 811 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.93 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY WEBSTER, HADDOCK, HOWARD, SOLOMON, HILL-EVANS, SANCHEZ, MADDEN, NEILSON, CERRATO AND GILLEN, MARCH 3, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON VETERANS AFFAIRS AND EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS, MARCH 3, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDirecting the Joint State Government Commission to conduct a comprehensive study and issue a report on Junior Reserve Officers' Training Corps programs in schools in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, The Junior Reserve Officers' Training Corps (JROTC) was created to instill in students the values of citizenship, service and personal responsibility; andWHEREAS, JROTC offers a curriculum that meets the common core State standards and provides lessons in leadership, health and wellness, physical fitness, first aid, geography, American history and government, communications and emotional intelligence; andWHEREAS, Each military branch offers its own JROTC program; andWHEREAS, School districts must apply to a specific military branch to implement a JROTC unit and commit to share costs and meet program requirements; andWHEREAS, A JROTC unit may not be established or maintained at a school unless certain enrollment requirements are met; and123456789101112131415161718 WHEREAS, Not all schools in this Commonwealth offer a JROTC program; andWHEREAS, JROTC programs can provide students with the opportunity to develop important life skills and gain exposure to military service, which they may choose to pursue in the future; andWHEREAS, Little information is known about the health and availability of JROTC programs in schools across this Commonwealth and what factors contribute to those trends; andWHEREAS, It is unclear what challenges school districts face when attempting to establish a JROTC unit, which may lead some school districts to choose not to pursue the establishment of a JROTC unit; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives direct the Joint State Government Commission to conduct a comprehensive study and issue a report on Junior Reserve Officers' Training Corps programs in schools in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That, at minimum, the study consist of an analysis of the following information:(1) the overall health of JROTC programs in Pennsylvania;(2) trends in enrollment and availability of JROTC programs; and(3) contributing factors to the health, enrollment and availability of JROTC programs;and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Joint State Government Commission submit a report of the study's findings with legislative recommendations to the General Assembly within 18 months of the adoption of this resolution.20250HR0093PN0811 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930
