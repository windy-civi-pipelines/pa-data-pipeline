2025-2026 Regular Session HB 1871 PN 2323 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 2323 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE BILL No.1871 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY CONKLIN, NEILSON, WAXMAN, HILL-EVANS, MERSKI, SANCHEZ AND GREEN, SEPTEMBER 18, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON COMMERCE, SEPTEMBER 22, 2025 AN ACTProviding for the installation of electrified security systems on commercial and industrial entities' property.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. Short title.This act shall be known and may be cited as the Commercial and Industrial Products and Property Protection Act.Section 2. Definitions.The following words and phrases when used in this act shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:"Commercial entity." An entity authorized to do business in this Commonwealth by being certified to conduct business by the Department of State."Electrified security system." An alarm system with an assembly of battery-charged equipment and all of its integrated components and equipment, including a monitored alarm device intended to notify the entity monitoring the alarm system of 123456789101112131415161718 possible intrusion to the property, an energizer which is intended to periodically deliver pulses to a security system, a battery charging device used exclusively to charge the system battery and any other integrated components. The term does not include a fence system used primarily for the containment of livestock or other animals."Municipality." A county, city, township, borough, incorporated town, home rule municipality or other general purpose unit of government established by an act of the General Assembly.Section 3. Electrified security system requirements.(a) Installation.--A commercial entity or industrial entity located in this Commonwealth may install an electrified security system in accordance with this act to protect property, products and any other commercial and industrial-related interests of the commercial entity or industrial entity from any unlawful actions.(b) Requirements for compliance.--An electrified security system installation shall:(1) Comply with the specifications in the 3.0 edition of the International Electrotechnical Commission Standard 60335-2-76.(2) Have an energizer that is driven by a commercial storage battery not to exceed 12 volts direct current.(3) Interface with a monitored alarm device that transmits a signal to the alarm monitoring company in response to an intrusion. The system may not directly connect to or call law enforcement.(4) Be located four inches to eight inches behind a nonelectrified perimeter barrier not less than five feet in 20250HB1871PN2323 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 height but not to exceed 10 feet in height, or two feet higher than the nonelectrified perimeter barrier, whichever is higher.(5) Be located on property that is not designated by a municipality exclusively for residential use.(6) Be clearly identified with warning signs containing the international electrified warning symbol at intervals of not greater than 30 linear feet.(c) Installer requirements.--The installer of an electrified security system shall provide notice to the municipality that the electrified security system has been installed pursuant to subsection (b) and the notice shall contain the alarm company installation name, the business entity name and address where the electrified security system was installed.(d) Local regulation.--A municipality may:(1) Require registration of an alarm system for the electrified security system.(2) Prohibit an electrified security system that does not meet the requirements of this section.Section 4. Construction.Nothing in this act shall be construed to apply to 18 Pa.C.S. ยง 7511(f) (relating to control of alarm devices and automatic dialing devices).Section 5. Effective date.This act shall take effect immediately.20250HB1871PN2323 - 3 - 12345678910111213141516171819202122232425
