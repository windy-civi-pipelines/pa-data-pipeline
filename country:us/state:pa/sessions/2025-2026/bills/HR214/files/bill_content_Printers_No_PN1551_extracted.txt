2025-2026 Regular Session HR 214 PN 1551 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1551 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.214 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY BRIGGS, KAUFFMAN, PIELLI, HANBIDGE, HOWARD, PICKETT, COOPER, D. WILLIAMS, SANCHEZ, HILL-EVANS AND NEILSON, APRIL 30, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY, APRIL 30, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the month of May 2025 as "National Treatment Court Month" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, All Rise, a national organization with 30 years of experience advocating for improvements in the justice system's response to substance use and mental health disorders, has designated the month of May as "National Treatment Court Month" to raise awareness of the effectiveness of treatment courts within the justice system; andWHEREAS, Treatment courts represent a significant advancement in the administration of justice by integrating evidence-based substance use disorder treatment, mental health services, accountability measures, recovery support services and intensive supervision with judicial oversight; andWHEREAS, These specialized court dockets address the root causes of criminal behavior, such as substance use disorders and mental health conditions, by focusing on treatment and recovery rather than punishment alone; and1234567891011121314151617 WHEREAS, Treatment courts in this Commonwealth, including adult and juvenile drug courts, DUI Courts, mental health courts, veterans treatment courts and family treatment courts, have demonstrated outstanding success in reducing recidivism, promoting recovery, preventing fatal overdoses and improving public safety; andWHEREAS, These courts offer a cost-effective alternative to incarceration by fostering community partnerships that unite public safety and public health efforts to build recovery capital within communities; andWHEREAS, The devoted professionals working in this Commonwealth's treatment courts, including judges, prosecutors, defense attorneys, probation officers, law enforcement personnel, treatment providers, case managers, peers, mentors and community volunteers, deserve commendation for their dedication to helping individuals reclaim their lives; andWHEREAS, Official recognition of "National Treatment Court Month" in this Commonwealth helps educate the public, policymakers and stakeholders on the critical role these courts play in addressing the opioid epidemic, the mental health crisis and other complex societal challenges; andWHEREAS, Continued support and expansion of treatment courts in this Commonwealth are vital to fostering healthier, safer and more resilient communities across this Commonwealth; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the month of May 2025 as "National Treatment Court Month" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives commend the dedicated professionals and volunteers who contribute to the 20250HR0214PN1551 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 success of treatment courts throughout this Commonwealth and encourage continued support for these vital programs; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be transmitted to the Governor, the Secretary of Drug and Alcohol Programs, the Supreme Court of Pennsylvania and All Rise.20250HR0214PN1551 - 3 - 123456
