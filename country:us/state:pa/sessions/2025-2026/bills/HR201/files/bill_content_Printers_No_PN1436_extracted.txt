2025-2026 Regular Session HR 201 PN 1436 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1436 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.201 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY HILL-EVANS, PASHINSKI, McNEILL, SANCHEZ, CONKLIN, K.HARRIS, HARKINS, PROBST, M. MACKENZIE, KRUPA, HADDOCK, DONAHUE, SOLOMON, VENKAT, NEILSON, FREEMAN, COOK, HOHENSTEIN, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ AND DOUGHERTY, APRIL 22, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON VETERANS AFFAIRS AND EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS, APRIL 22, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing September 29, 2025, as "VFW Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, The Veterans of Foreign Wars of the United States, known as the VFW, is a nonprofit veterans service organization comprised of eligible veterans and military service members from the active, guard and reserve forces; andWHEREAS, The VFW was established on September 29, 1899, by a small group of veterans of different foreign wars who called themselves the American Veterans of Foreign Service and later merged with two other combat war veteran associations during a historic convention near Pittsburgh, becoming the Veterans of Foreign Wars of the United States and opening their membership to veterans from all foreign wars and branches of service; andWHEREAS, The mission of the VFW is to foster camaraderie among United States veterans of overseas conflicts, serve our veterans, the military and their communities, and advocate on behalf of all veterans; and12345678910111213141516 WHEREAS, Since its inception, the VFW has been a leading advocate for veterans' rights and benefits, playing a crucial role in the establishment of the Veterans Administration (VA), the national veterans' cemetery system and the GI Bill of Rights; andWHEREAS, The VFW was instrumental in the passage of the Sergeant First Class Heath Robinson Honoring our Promise to Address Comprehensive Toxics Act of 2022 signed into law by President Biden, which expanded and extended eligibility for VA health care for veterans with toxic exposures and veterans of the Vietnam War, Gulf War and post-9/11 eras, and added more than 20 more presumptive conditions for burn pits, Agent Orange and other toxic exposures, while making other improvements for the health and benefit of our veterans; andWHEREAS, The VFW and its Auxiliary boast a membership of more than 1.4 million individuals, with approximately 6,000 Posts worldwide; andWHEREAS, The VFW has provided tens of millions of dollars in financial assistance to military families, scholarships and local community service projects; andWHEREAS, The VFW helps veterans recoup billions of dollars annually in the United States Department of Veterans Affairs disability compensation and pension benefits, ensuring veterans and their families receive the care they earned and deserve; andWHEREAS, The VFW continues to honor its motto, "No One Does More For Veterans," through unwavering devotion and service to veterans, military personnel and communities; andWHEREAS, "VFW Day" is a time to commemorate the hard work and sacrifice of veteran men and women who have selflessly served the United States of America, and to recognize the continued 20250HR0201PN1436 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 service and dedication of the VFW; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize September 29, 2025, as "VFW Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That all Pennsylvanians are encouraged to honor and support the members and veterans of the VFW and their families for their invaluable contributions to our nation and communities.20250HR0201PN1436 - 3 - 12345678
