2025-2026 Regular Session SR 151 PN 1160 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1160 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIASENATE RESOLUTION No.151 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY HUTCHINSON, LAUGHLIN, MASTRIANO, BROWN, ROTHMAN, FARRY, FONTANA, MARTIN, SANTARSIERO, VOGEL, J. WARD, CULVER, CAPPELLETTI, COSTA, STEFANO, ROBINSON AND DUSH, SEPTEMBER 10, 2025 INTRODUCED AND ADOPTED, SEPTEMBER 10, 2025 A RESOLUTIONHonoring the life, service and memory of United States Army Air Forces Corporal Glenn Hodak.WHEREAS, Glenn Hodak was born March 17, 1922, in Cambridge Springs, Pennsylvania; andWHEREAS, Mr. Hodak enlisted into the United States Army Air Forces, to courageously serve this Commonwealth and Country in World War II, achieving the rank of corporal; andWHEREAS, Corporal Hodak became a member of the 93rd Bombardment Squadron, 19th Bombardment Group, flying in a B-29 Superfortress Bomber, nicknamed the "Tall in the Saddle"; andWHEREAS, Corporal Hodak was positioned as a tail gunner and deployed for the Great Tokyo Air Raid on March 10, 1945; andWHEREAS, The "Tall in the Saddle" flew with 300 American B-29 Superfortress bombers to drop 1,700 tons of incendiary bombs on Tokyo, Japan; andWHEREAS, Corporal Hodak and his crew members were shot down and became prisoners of war until May 26, 1945, when Corporal 1234567891011121314151617 Hodak and others tragically perished in the May 1945 Tokyo Prison Fire; andWHEREAS, After the fire, the American Graves Registration Service concluded that the remains represented 64 deceased service members but only identified 25 through dental records; andWHEREAS, The remaining 39 servicemen, including Corporal Glenn Hodak, were unidentified for over 80 years and laid to rest at Manila American Cemetery and Memorial in Fort Bonifacio, Taguig City, Philippines; andWHEREAS, In April 2022, these servicemen were disinterred and sent to a Defense POW/MIA Accounting Agency laboratory where scientists, using dental records and anthropological analysis, identified Corporal Hodak and notified his family on September 25, 2024; andWHEREAS, Corporal Hodak's remains will return to the Commonwealth with a military escort and be buried in Spring Creek Cemetery with his mother and two brothers; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the Senate honor the life, service and memory of United States Army Air Forces Corporal Glenn Hodak; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Senate observe, with dignity, Corporal Hodak's return to this Commonwealth to be put to rest with his family; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Senate offer condolences to his loved ones.20250SR0151PN1160 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223242526
