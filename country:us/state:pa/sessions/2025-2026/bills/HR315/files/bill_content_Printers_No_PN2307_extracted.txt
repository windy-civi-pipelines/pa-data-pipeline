2025-2026 Regular Session HR 315 PN 2307 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 2307 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.315 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY HOGAN, GILLEN AND ROWE, SEPTEMBER 11, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON HEALTH, SEPTEMBER 11, 2025 A RESOLUTIONUrging the Congress of the United States and the United States Department of Veterans Affairs to enable veterans to access hyperbaric oxygen therapy for both traumatic brain injury and post-traumatic stress disorder.WHEREAS, The courageous men and women who have served in the United States Armed Forces have made immense sacrifices to protect our nation and uphold its values; andWHEREAS, Many veterans suffer from traumatic brain injury and post-traumatic stress disorder as a result of their service, significantly impacting their quality of life and overall well-being; andWHEREAS, Hyperbaric oxygen therapy is a promising and powerful procedure in treating both traumatic brain injury and post-traumatic stress disorder by increasing oxygen levels in the body, promoting healing and reducing inflammation; andWHEREAS, Despite its potential benefits, hyperbaric oxygen therapy remains limited for veterans due to various barriers, including lack of awareness, availability and limited insurance coverage; and12345678910111213141516171819 WHEREAS, The United States Department of Veterans Affairs currently offers hyperbaric oxygen therapy as a treatment for a limited number of veterans with persistent post-traumatic stress disorder symptoms; andWHEREAS, Ongoing advocacy by American Legion, Vietnam Veterans of America, AMVETS and other organizations seeks to widen availability of hyperbaric oxygen therapy for more veterans, recognizing its advantages; andWHEREAS, United States Congressman Gregory Murphy proposed legislation (H.R. 1336), known as the "Veterans National Traumatic Brain Injury Treatment Act," which proposes a five-year pilot program for hyperbaric oxygen therapy for veterans suffering from traumatic brain injury and post-traumatic stress disorder; andWHEREAS, Providing veterans with greater access to hyperbaric oxygen therapy could significantly improve their health outcomes and enhance their ability to reintegrate into civilian life; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania urge the Congress of the United States and the United States Department of Veterans Affairs to enable veterans to access hyperbaric oxygen therapy for both traumatic brain injury and post-traumatic stress disorder; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be transmitted to the United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs, the presiding officers of each house of Congress and each member of Congress from Pennsylvania.20250HR0315PN2307 - 2 - 12345678910111213141516171819202122232425262728
