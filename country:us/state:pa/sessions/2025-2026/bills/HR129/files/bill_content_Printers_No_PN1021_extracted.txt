2025-2026 Regular Session HR 129 PN 1021 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1021 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.129 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY BARGER, MARCH 18, 2025 INTRODUCED AS NONCONTROVERSIAL RESOLUTION UNDER RULE 35, MARCH 18, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the service of the Honorable Jerry A. Stern, an 11-term former member of the House of Representatives, and expressing condolences upon his death on December 11, 2024, in Martinsburg, Blair County, at 69 years of age.WHEREAS, The House of Representatives mourns the loss of Jerry A. Stern, who served the 80th District with distinction, representing Bedford and Blair Counties from 1993 through 2002 and Blair County from 2003 through January 6, 2015; andWHEREAS, With integrity and purpose, Mr. Stern championed agriculture, tourism promotion, library and school funding and employment; andWHEREAS, With those same values, Mr. Stern mentored other members of the General Assembly; andWHEREAS, From 2007 through 2010, Mr. Stern earned respect for his leadership as Minority Caucus Secretary; andWHEREAS, From 2011 through 2014, Mr. Stern provided leadership as chair of the Tourism and Recreational Development Committee; andWHEREAS, Mr. Stern further served the General Assembly and 12345678910111213141516171819 the residents of this Commonwealth as an appointed member of the Pennsylvania Commission on Crime and Delinquency, the Task Force on Commonwealth-Owned Lands and the Legislative Data Processing Center Commission; andWHEREAS, Born January 11, 1955, in Roaring Spring, Blair County, Mr. Stern was a son of the late Merle and Vera Replogle Stern; andWHEREAS, Mr. Stern was a graduate of Central High School in Martinsburg and a former Wineland IGA manager; andWHEREAS, At the Blair County Courthouse, Mr. Stern worked as deputy prothonotary from 1982 through 1989 and prothonotary and clerk of courts from 1989 through his 1992 election to the House of Representatives; andWHEREAS, The Hollidaysburg American Legion Ambulance Service, Hollidaysburg Veterans Home, Nason Foundation, Blair County Farm Bureau, American Cancer Society, Martinsburg Rotary Club, Martinsburg Sportsmen's Association, Cobras Softball Team and other organizations benefited from Mr. Stern's energies and experience; andWHEREAS, A lifelong member of Martinsburg Grace Brethren Church, Mr. Stern is remembered for his faithful service to his congregation and community; andWHEREAS, The House of Representatives remembers Mr. Stern's contributions and achievements as a lawmaker and mentor; andWHEREAS, Mr. Stern's wife Susan E. Stern, his children Jason Stern and Gretchen Stern Bettwy and his grandchildren survive him; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the service of the Honorable Jerry A. Stern, an 11-term former member of the House of Representatives, and express condolences 20250HR0129PN1021 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 upon his death on December 11, 2024, in Martinsburg, Blair County, at 69 years of age; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That a copy of this resolution be transmitted to the family of Jerry A.
