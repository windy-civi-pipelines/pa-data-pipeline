2025-2026 Regular Session SR 142 PN 1132 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1132 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIASENATE RESOLUTION No.142 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY FLYNN, J. WARD, TARTAGLIONE, KEARNEY, MALONE, MARTIN, BROWN, HUGHES, PHILLIPS-HILL, CULVER, FONTANA, KANE AND CAPPELLETTI, SEPTEMBER 4, 2025 REFERRED TO RULES AND EXECUTIVE NOMINATIONS, SEPTEMBER 4, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating September 1, 2025, as "Drive Sober or Get Pulled Over Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Driving under the influence of alcohol, drugs or both continues to pose a serious threat to public safety in Pennsylvania and across the United States; andWHEREAS, According to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), more than 12,000 people lose their lives in alcohol-impaired traffic crashes annually, representing nearly one-third of all traffic fatalities in the United States; andWHEREAS, During the 2023 Labor Day weekend, 511 people died across the nation in traffic crashes; andWHEREAS, In Pennsylvania, impaired driving-related crashes remain a persistent concern, resulting in avoidable injuries and loss of life each year; andWHEREAS, Alcohol-impaired driving killed 321 people in Pennsylvania during 2023, accounting for 27% of Pennsylvania's 1234567891011121314151617 traffic deaths; andWHEREAS, There were 1,002 crashes Statewide over the 2024 Labor Day weekend, resulting in 13 fatalities and 722 injuries; andWHEREAS, The "Drive Sober or Get Pulled Over" campaign during the Labor Day weekend is a national initiative led by NHTSA that combines high-visibility enforcement with public awareness efforts to deter impaired driving and save lives; andWHEREAS, The Department of Transportation of the Commonwealth and Pennsylvania law enforcement agencies participate in this campaign annually to emphasize the message that impaired driving is both illegal and dangerous; andWHEREAS, Designating a specific day in recognition of the campaign enhances public awareness, supports Statewide and local enforcement efforts and reinforces Pennsylvania's commitment to promoting safe and sober driving practices; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the Senate designate September 1, 2025, as "Drive Sober or Get Pulled Over Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Senate recognize the importance of the campaign's message, commend the efforts of law enforcement agencies across this Commonwealth and urge all Pennsylvanians to make responsible choices by never driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs.20250SR0142PN1132 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324
