The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. The definition of "best management practice" or
"BMP" in section 1601-R of the act of April 9, 1929 (P.L.343,
No.176), known as The Fiscal Code, is amended and the section is
amended by adding definitions to read:
Section 1601-R. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this article
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:
* * *
"Best management practice" or "BMP." A practice or
combination of practices determined by the commission or United
States Department of Agriculture Natural Resources Conservation
Service to be effective and practical, considering
technological, economic and institutional factors, to manage
nutrients and sediment to protect surface water and groundwater.
The term includes the use of cover crops.
* * *
"Cover crop." A crop grown primarily for the purpose of
controlling erosion, suppressing weeds, reducing soil
compaction, increasing moisture and nutrient content of soil,
improving yield potential, attracting pollinators or providing
habitat for beneficial insects and wildlife as well as
supplemental food for animals, including certain grasses, small
grains and legumes.
"Cover crop project." A best management practice in which
cover crops are used.

Section 2. Section 1604-R(e) of the act is amended by adding
a paragraph to read:
Section 1604-R. Powers, duties and administration.
* * *
(e) Powers and duties of participating conservation
districts.--The following shall apply:
* * *
(2.1) (i) Except as provided under subparagraph (ii),
no less than 15% of the amount apportioned to each
conservation district shall be awarded for cover crop
projects.
(ii) If a conservation district receives complete
applications for cover crop projects in amounts less than
the amount under subparagraph (i), the conservation
district may allocate the remaining amount to other best
management practices.
* * *
Section 3. This act shall take effect in 60 days.
