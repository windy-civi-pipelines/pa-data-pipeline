2025-2026 Regular Session HR 240 PN 2290 Bill Text (HTM) PRINTER'S NO. 2290 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.240 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY MARCELL, LABS, MAJOR, MIHALEK, TOMLINSON, ARMANINI, STAATS AND GILLEN, SEPTEMBER 5, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY, SEPTEMBER 9, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the month of January 2026 as "Stalking Awareness Month" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Stalking is a significant issue in the United States that affects millions of people every year; andWHEREAS, According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, approximately one in three women and one in six men in the United States experience stalking at some point in their lives; andWHEREAS, The Stalking Prevention, Awareness and Resource Center estimates that 13.5 million people are stalked every year in the United States; andWHEREAS, The majority of stalking victims in the United States are threatened with physical harm; andWHEREAS, With the rise of digital technology and the use of social media platforms, cyberstalking has become increasingly prevalent enabling stalkers to monitor, harass and intimidate victims from a distance often anonymously and across various online spaces; and123456789101112131415161718 WHEREAS, Stalking victims often experience heightened anxiety, fear and a pervasive sense of dread; andWHEREAS, The stress and trauma experienced by victims of stalking can lead to depression, feelings of hopelessness, sadness and withdrawal from social interactions; andWHEREAS, January 2024 marked the 20th annual National Stalking Awareness Month, an annual call to action to recognize and counter the crime of stalking; andWHEREAS, Public recognition can empower stalking victims by validating their experiences and encouraging them to report stalking behavior to law enforcement; andWHEREAS, By recognizing January 2026 as "Stalking Awareness Month," the General Assembly can inform and educate the public about the crime of stalking, its prevalence in this Commonwealth, its impact on victims and the various resources and support systems available to assist and protect victims; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the month of January 2026 as "Stalking Awareness Month" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives commit to raising awareness and providing resources to support and protect victims of stalking in this Commonwealth.20250HR0240PN2290 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223
