2025-2026 Regular Session HR 2 PN 0033 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 33 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.2 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY WATRO, CONKLIN, PICKETT, LEADBETER, KAUFFMAN, HAMM, VENKAT, JAMES, NEILSON, HADDOCK, DELLOSO, FREEMAN, ROWE AND REICHARD, JANUARY 10, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON VETERANS AFFAIRS AND EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS, JANUARY 10, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing March 25, 2025, as "National Medal of Honor Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, The Medal of Honor, the highest award for valor in action against an enemy force, is bestowed upon members of the armed forces who have distinguished themselves conspicuously by gallantry and intrepidity at great personal risk, going above and beyond the call of duty; andWHEREAS, The Medal of Honor was first awarded on March 25, 1863, and has since been conferred to 3,517 individuals for their bravery and selflessness; andWHEREAS, There are currently 61 living recipients of the Medal of Honor; andWHEREAS, Pennsylvania has a rich legacy of valor, with 379 accredited Medal of Honor recipients, ranking second only to New York state in total awards; andWHEREAS, Pennsylvania takes immense pride in its distinguished military history and the courageous individuals 1234567891011121314151617 who have selflessly served our nation; andWHEREAS, To honor these exceptional heroes, the Pennsylvania General Assembly has designated a portion of United States Route 20, spanning from the Pennsylvania-Ohio State Line to the Pennsylvania-New York State Line, as the "Pennsylvania Medal of Honor Highway"; andWHEREAS, This highway segment serves as a lasting tribute to the extraordinary courage and unwavering commitment of our Medal of Honor recipients; andWHEREAS, It is important to commemorate and honor the recipients of the Medal of Honor; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize March 25, 2025, as "National Medal of Honor Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives wholeheartedly support the goals and ideals of a "National Medal of Honor Day" to celebrate the contributions of Medal of Honor recipients; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives commemorate the heroism and sacrifice of Medal of Honor recipients and recognize March 25, 2025, as "National Medal of Honor Day" in Pennsylvania.20250HR0002PN0033 - 2 - 12345678910111213141516171819202122
