2025-2026 Regular Session HB 1066 PN 1167 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1167 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE BILL No.1066 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY BURGOS, KAZEEM, HILL-EVANS, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, GUENST, GIRAL, PIELLI, SANCHEZ, HOHENSTEIN, SCHLOSSBERG, MALAGARI, D. WILLIAMS, GREEN AND MAYES, MARCH 31, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON PROFESSIONAL LICENSURE, MARCH 31, 2025 AN ACTAmending the act of December 20, 1985 (P.L.457, No.112), entitled "An act relating to the right to practice medicine and surgery and the right to practice medically related acts; reestablishing the State Board of Medical Education and Licensure as the State Board of Medicine and providing for its composition, powers and duties; providing for the issuance of licenses and certificates and the suspension and revocation of licenses and certificates; provided penalties; and making repeals," further providing for temporary license.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. Section 33(a) introductory paragraph of the act of December 20, 1985 (P.L.457, No.112), known as the Medical Practice Act of 1985, is amended and the section is amended by adding a subsection to read:Section 33. Temporary license.(a) General rule.--[A] Except as provided under subsection (d), a temporary license empowers the licensee to:* * *(d) Exception.--The board shall grant a full and unrestricted license to practice medicine to a temporary 123456789101112131415161718192021 licensee not subject to subsection (a) who is a graduate from an international medical school, performed the duties of a physician for at least three years outside of the United States and demonstrates all of the following:(1) C ompetency as determined by the board. (2) Good standing with the board two years after the date of issuance of a temporary license from the board.(3) Sufficient evidence that the temporary licensee has an offer of employment as a physician at a health care facility that operates in this Commonwealth.Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.20250HB1066PN1167 - 2 - 1234567891011
