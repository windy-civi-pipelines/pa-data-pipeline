4701. Scope of chapter.
This chapter relates to the award of Buy America, Buy Union
grants.
4702. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this chapter
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Authority." As defined in 53 Pa.C.S. 5602 (relating to
definitions).
"Bus." As defined in 75 Pa.C.S. 102 (relating to
definitions).
"Business entity." A corporation, partnership, limited
liability company, business trust, association, estate, trust,
foundation or similar entity.
"Department." The Department of Community and Economic
Development of the Commonwealth.
"Fund." The Buy America, Buy Union Grant Fund established
under section 4721 (relating to establishment of fund).
"Labor organization." As follows:
(1) Except as provided in paragraph (2), as defined in
section 3(f) of the act of June 1, 1937 (P.L.1168, No.294),
known as the Pennsylvania Labor Relations Act.
(2) The term does not include an organization in which
more than one-half of the employees of the organization are
owners, officers or executives of the organization.
"Passenger car." As defined in 75 Pa.C.S. 102.
"Procure." As follows:

"School bus." As defined in 75 Pa.C.S. 102.

The Buy America, Buy Union Grant Program is established
within the department.
4712. Operation of program.
The department shall implement and administer the program for
the purpose of providing grants in accordance with this chapter
to reimburse political subdivisions and authorities that procure
vehicles which are union-made.
4713. Application.
(a) Form.--
(1) The department shall develop an application form for
political subdivisions and authorities to use to seek a grant
under the program.
(2) The application form under paragraph (1) may specify
the vehicles that would be eligible for a grant under the
program.
(3) The department shall post the application form under
paragraph (1) on the publicly accessible Internet website of
the department.
(b) Contents.--An applicant seeking a grant under the
program shall provide the following information to the
department:
(1) The name and contact information of the applicant.
(2) The amount of the grant requested.
(3) The number of vehicles which are union-made for
which a grant under the program is sought, including a
description of the vehicles.
(4) Evidence of procurement and union-made status.
(5) Any other information deemed necessary by the
department.
(c) Submittal.--The department shall prescribe the process

4714. Limitations.
(a) Amount of grants.--The amount of a grant under the
program for an applicant shall be limited to not more than 20%
of the costs incurred by the applicant regarding the procurement
of the vehicles which are union-made.
(b) Distribution of grants.--Grants under the program shall
be disbursed on a first-come, first-served basis determined by
the department and until all money in the fund is exhausted.
4715. Determination.
(a) Review.--The department shall review each completed
application for a grant under the program and shall determine
whether to award the grant.
(b) Notice.--The department shall provide notice of the
determination under subsection (a). The following shall apply
regarding the notice:
(1) If the department awards the grant, the department
shall include the amount of the grant and any conditions on
the use of the grant.
(2) If the department does not award the grant in whole
or in part, the department shall include the reasons for the
full or partial denial.

The Buy America, Buy Union Grant Fund is established in the
State Treasury to carry out the purposes of the program.
4722. Operation of fund.
(a) Appropriation.--All money deposited into the fund and
interest and other earnings on the money in the fund are
appropriated to the department on a continuing basis to award
grants under the program.
(b) End of fiscal year.--Any money remaining in the fund at
the end of each fiscal year, including interest and other
earnings, shall not revert to the General Fund but shall remain
in the fund.
4723. Funding source.
The department may accept funding for the program, including
money appropriated by the General Assembly, Federal money,
grants, donations, gifts and other payments from any source,
which shall be deposited into the fund.
