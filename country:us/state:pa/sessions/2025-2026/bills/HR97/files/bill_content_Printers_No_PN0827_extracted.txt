2025-2026 Regular Session HR 97 PN 0827 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 827 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.97 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY RUSNOCK, REICHARD, McNEILL, HILL-EVANS, VENKAT, GIRAL, KHAN, FREEMAN, SCHLOSSBERG, CERRATO, DONAHUE, SANCHEZ, MALAGARI, COOPER AND PUGH, MARCH 3, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON LABOR AND INDUSTRY, MARCH 3, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the week of May 4 through 10, 2025, as "Career and Technology Week" and recognizing May 7, 2025, as "National Skilled Trades Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Career and technical education provides students with academic knowledge, technical skills and hands-on training in a wide range of fields, including health care, information technology, manufacturing, construction and public services, preparing them for both college and career pathways; andWHEREAS, Skilled trades and technical fields play a critical role in driving economic growth, meeting workforce demands and improving the quality of life for communities across Pennsylvania; andWHEREAS, There are more than 80 career and technical education centers across Pennsylvania that offer approved programs to thousands of students, providing those students with the necessary skills and training to succeed in high-demand occupations; andWHEREAS, Pennsylvania, like much of the nation, is 123456789101112131415161718 experiencing a shortage of skilled workers in essential industries, including manufacturing, construction, health care, information technology and transportation, making investment in career and technical education programs critical to sustaining and growing this Commonwealth's economy; andWHEREAS, "National Skilled Trades Day" is observed annually on the first Wednesday in May to honor the contributions of skilled trades professionals and to raise awareness about the value of skilled trades careers; andWHEREAS, Aligning "Career and Technology Week" with "National Skilled Trades Day" creates a unified Statewide celebration of career and technical education, highlighting the contributions of educators, students and professionals who advance technical and skilled trades fields; andWHEREAS, Recognizing the importance of career and technical education and skilled trades at a Statewide level encourages students to explore diverse pathways, bridges the gap between education and industry and provides valuable opportunities for personal and professional growth; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the week of May 4 through 10, 2025, as "Career and Technology Week" and recognize May 7, 2025, as "National Skilled Trades Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the vital contributions of career and technical education and skilled trades professionals to Pennsylvania's economy and workforce.20250HR0097PN0827 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627
