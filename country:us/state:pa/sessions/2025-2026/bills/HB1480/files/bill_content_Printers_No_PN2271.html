§ 2501. Scope of chapter.
This chapter relates to the nonpublic database registry of
domestic violence offenders.
§ 2502. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this chapter
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Database registry." The Nonpublic Database Registry for
Domestic Violence Offenders established under section 2511
(relating to establishment).
"Family or household members." As defined in 23 Pa.C.S. §
6102(a) (relating to definitions).
"Law enforcement agency." Any of the following:
(1) The Pennsylvania State Police.
(2) A police department of a municipality.
(3) Any other entity that employs individuals who, by
virtue of their office or public employment, are vested by
law with a duty to maintain public order or make arrests for
offenses, whether the duty extends to all offenses or is
limited to specific offenses.
(4) The Office of Attorney General.
(5) A county district attorney's office.
"Municipality." A county, city, borough, incorporated town
or township of this Commonwealth.

§ 2511. Establishment.
The Nonpublic Database Registry for Domestic Violence
Offenders is established to carry out the purposes of this
chapter.
§ 2512. Implementation and administration.
The Pennsylvania State Police shall implement and administer
the database registry.
§ 2513. Purpose.
The purpose of the database registry shall be to maintain a
complete and systematic index of all records regarding domestic
violence offenders in this Commonwealth.
§ 2514. Accessibility.
The database registry shall be accessible only by a law
enforcement agency and the Office of Victim Advocate.
§ 2515. Characteristics generally.
The database registry shall be:
(1) Composed of an electronic database and digitized
records.
(2) Able to communicate with analogous registries
established in other jurisdictions.

§ 2517. In-person reporting.
(a) Periodic in-person appearance required.--Except as
provided in subsections (b), (c) and (d), an individual who is
required to register in accordance with section 2516(a)
(relating to registration) shall appear in person at an approved
registration site established by the Pennsylvania State Police
to provide or verify the information specified in section
2518(a) (relating to information) and be photographed. The
following apply:
(1) If the individual registered as a result of murder
or voluntary manslaughter, the individual shall appear on a
quarterly basis.
(2) If the individual registered as a result of
involuntary manslaughter, aggravated assault, strangulation,
stalking or terroristic threats, the individual shall appear
on a semiannual basis.
(3) If the individual registered as a result of simple
assault or recklessly endangering another person, the
individual shall appear on an annual basis.
(b) Transient.--If an individual who is required to register
in accordance with section 2516(a) is a transient, the following
apply:

§ 2518. Information.
(a) Provided by domestic violence offender.--An individual
who is required to register in accordance with section 2516(a)
(relating to registration) shall provide the following
information for inclusion in the database registry:
(1) The primary or given name of the individual,
including an alias, nickname, pseudonym or ethnic or tribal
name, regardless of the context used, and any designation or
moniker used by the individual for self-identification in
Internet communications or postings.
(2) Any designation used by the individual for purposes
of routing or self-identification in Internet communications
or postings.
(3) The telephone number, including cell phone number,
and any other designation used by the individual for purposes
of routing or self-identification in telephonic
communications.

§ 2519. Penalties.
An individual who is subject to registration under this
chapter commits a misdemeanor of the first degree if the
individual knowingly fails to:
(1) Register with the Pennsylvania State Police as
required under this chapter.
(2) Verify the address of the individual or be
photographed as required under this chapter.
(3) Provide accurate information when registering and
periodically appearing in person as required under this
chapter.
Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.
