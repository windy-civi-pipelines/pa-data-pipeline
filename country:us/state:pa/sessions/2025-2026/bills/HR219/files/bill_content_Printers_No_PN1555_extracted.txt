JONES, SCHLOSSBERG, KENYATTA, DALEY, GREEN, MADDEN AND CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, APRIL 30, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON INTERGOVERNMENTAL AFFAIRS AND OPERATIONS, APRIL 30, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating July 17, 2025, as "100th anniversary of American Legion Baseball Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, American Legion Baseball is one of the most successful amateur sports leagues in the country, surviving through World War II and other national tragedies; andWHEREAS, Players between 13 and 19 years of age, in all 50 states and Canada, can compete in this league with the hopes of eventually playing college or professional baseball; andWHEREAS, By participating in American Legion Baseball, players learn about teamwork, discipline, good sportsmanship and leadership, encouraging today's youth to become tomorrow's leaders; andWHEREAS, The league awards more than $25,000 in college scholarships to its players each year based off of their character, leadership, scholarship and financial need; andWHEREAS, Over the last 100 years, 82 former American Legion 12345678910111213141516 Baseball players have been inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame; andWHEREAS, The first American Legion Baseball World Series took place in Philadelphia in 1926, including teams from 15 states; andWHEREAS, In 1982, Boyertown became the first American Legion Baseball World Series Champion from Pennsylvania, and the team's 1987 win continues to be the second-most dominant championship run in the league's history; andWHEREAS, As of 2024, there are 305 American Legion Baseball teams in Pennsylvania, which is the second-highest number of teams compared to all states across the country; andWHEREAS, It is important for all Pennsylvanians to recognize the value of American Legion Baseball to this Commonwealth and our nation's pastime; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives designate July 17, 2025, as "100th anniversary of American Legion Baseball Day" in Pennsylvania.20250HR0219PN1555 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718
