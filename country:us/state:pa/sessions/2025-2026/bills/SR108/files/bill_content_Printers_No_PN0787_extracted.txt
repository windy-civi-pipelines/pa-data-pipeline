2025-2026 Regular Session SR 108 PN 0787 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 787 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIASENATE RESOLUTION No.108 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY CULVER, FARRY, BROOKS, FONTANA, BROWN, MARTIN, SCHWANK, VOGEL, STEFANO, COMITTA, ROBINSON, COSTA, ARGALL, HUGHES, KANE, MASTRIANO AND J. WARD, MAY 12, 2025 REFERRED TO RULES AND EXECUTIVE NOMINATIONS, MAY 12, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating May 13, 2025, as "Cancer Action Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, The risk of dying from cancer has steadily declined over the last 30 years largely because of smoking cessation, early cancer detection and advances in the treatment of cancer and precision medicine; andWHEREAS, More advances must be made as the American Cancer Society estimates that in 2025 there will be 2 million new cases of cancer in the United States; andWHEREAS, More than 618,000 people will lose their lives to cancer in 2025; andWHEREAS, Pennsylvania's cancer incidence and mortality rates continue to be higher than the national rates, with a predicted 90,000 Pennsylvanians to face a new cancer diagnosis in 2025 and an estimated 27,500 Commonwealth residents to lose their fight against cancer this year; andWHEREAS, In this Commonwealth, the five most common new cases 1234567891011121314151617 of cancer estimated in 2025 include breast cancer at 13,650 cases, prostate cancer at 13,400 cases, lung cancer at 10,250 cases, colorectal cancer at 6,500 cases and urinary bladder cancer at 4,150 cases; andWHEREAS, Many of these cancers are often preventable, and early detection and treatment are critical as cancer does not always cause symptoms and many cancers occur in people with no family history; andWHEREAS, The American Cancer Society Cancer Action Network volunteers are gathering at the Pennsylvania Capitol on May 13, 2025, for "Cancer Action Day," in coalition with allied partners to advance legislative priorities that increase Pennsylvanians' access to quality health care and cancer prevention services; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the Senate designate May 13, 2025, as "Cancer Action Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Senate honor all Pennsylvanians impacted by cancer and those who devote their lives to fighting this disease.20250SR0108PN0787 - 2 - 12345678910111213141516171819
