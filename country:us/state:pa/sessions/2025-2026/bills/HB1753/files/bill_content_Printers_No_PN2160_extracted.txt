2025-2026 Regular Session HB 1753 PN 2160 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 2160 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE BILL No.1753 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY FLOOD, BOROWSKI, MAKO, NEILSON, PICKETT AND PROBST, JULY 22, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON CONSUMER PROTECTION, TECHNOLOGY AND UTILITIES, JULY 23, 2025 AN ACTAmending Titles 18 (Crimes and Offenses) and 66 (Public Utilities) of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes, in public utilities, providing for transportation network service offenses; and, in transportation network service, further providing for definitions and for transportation network company drivers.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. Title 18 of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes is amended by adding a section to read:§ 6911. Transportation network service offenses. (a) Match verification.--An individual who is a transportation network company driver and fails to use match verification as required under 66 Pa.C.S. § 2605(d) (relating to t ransportation network company drivers) is guilty of a summary offense.(b) False representation for inducement to enter.--An individual who falsely represents that the individual is a transportation network company driver for the purpose of 12345678910111213141516171819 inducing another individual to enter a vehicle shall be guilty of a misdemeanor of the second degree.(c) False representation for inducement resulting in bodily harm.--An individual who falsely represents that the individual is a transportation network company driver for the purpose of inducing an individual to enter a vehicle, resulting in bodily injury to the other individual, is guilty of a misdemeanor of the first degree.(d) Definition.--As used in this section, the term "t ransportation network company driver" shall have the same meaning given to it in 66 Pa.C.S. § 102 (relating to definitions).Section 2. Section 2601 of Title 66 is amended by adding a definition to read:§ 2601. Definitions.The following words and phrases when used in this chapter shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:* * *"Match verification." A process by which a transportation network company uses a personal identification number, device proximity or other system to confirm that an intended transportation network company passenger is entering a vehicle operated by the intended transportation network company driver.Section 3. Section 2605 of Title 66 is amended by adding a subsection to read:§ 2605. Transportation network company drivers.* * *(d) Match verification required.--A transportation network company driver shall use match verification for any prearranged 20250HB1753PN2160 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 ride requested by an individual through the transportation network company's digital network.Section 4.
