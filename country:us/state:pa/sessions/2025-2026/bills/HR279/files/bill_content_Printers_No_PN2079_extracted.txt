WILLIAMS, JULY 1, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY, JULY 1, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the month of October 2025 as "Domestic Violence Awareness Month" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Beginning in 1989, the month of October was declared as "National Domestic Violence Awareness Month" in the United States; andWHEREAS, The Department of Justice defines "domestic violence" as "a pattern of abusive behavior in any relationship that is used by one partner to gain or maintain power and control over another intimate partner"; andWHEREAS, Domestic violence is a significant issue in the United States that affects more than 10 million people every year; andWHEREAS, According to the Pennsylvania Coalition Against Domestic Violence (PCADV), one in three women and one in four men experience domestic violence in their lifetimes; andWHEREAS, Over the past decade, PCADV reports that more than 1,400 individuals were killed in this Commonwealth as a result 1234567891011121314151617 of domestic violence; andWHEREAS, In 2024 alone, 104 individuals died as a result of domestic violence in this Commonwealth according to PCADV; andWHEREAS, Across the United States, PCADV reports that 20% of pregnant women experience domestic violence during their pregnancy; andWHEREAS, Women with unintended pregnancies are two to four times more likely to experience physical abuse compared to those with planned pregnancies; andWHEREAS, Homicide is the leading cause of death among pregnant women in the United States; andWHEREAS, The stress and trauma experienced by victims of domestic violence can lead to depression, feelings of hopelessness, sadness and withdrawal from social interactions; andWHEREAS, Domestic violence disrupts the family structure and creates an environment that breeds fear, mistrust and instability; andWHEREAS, Domestic violence normalizes unhealthy and abusive relationships, leading to confusion with respect to appropriate behavior and boundaries within a family; andWHEREAS, According to the National Institutes of Health, children who are exposed to domestic violence commonly grow up to believe that violence is a reasonable approach to resolving conflicts; andWHEREAS, Various groups dedicated to providing resources and support services for victims of domestic violence served more than 3,000 victims in a single day in 2023; andWHEREAS, PCADV estimates that the lifetime economic burden of intimate partner violence in this Commonwealth is $156 billion; 20250HR0279PN2079 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 andWHEREAS, PCADV's publicly accessible Internet website lists various resources and support services for victims of domestic violence throughout this Commonwealth; andWHEREAS, The Office of Victim Advocate established the Address Confidentiality Program for victims of domestic and sexual violence as required by 23 Pa.C.S. ยง 6703; andWHEREAS, Domestic violence public awareness can empower victims by validating their experiences and encouraging them to report acts of domestic violence to law enforcement before the violence escalates; andWHEREAS, By recognizing the month of October 2025 as "Domestic Violence Awareness Month," the House of Representatives can inform and educate the public about domestic violence, its prevalence in this Commonwealth, its impact on victims and families and the various resources and support systems available to assist and protect victims; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the month of October 2025 as "Domestic Violence Awareness Month" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives commit to raising awareness and providing resources to support and protect victims of domestic violence in this Commonwealth.20250HR0279PN2079 - 3 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223
