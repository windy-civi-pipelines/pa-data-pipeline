2025-2026 Regular Session HR 70 PN 0560 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 560 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.70 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY T. JONES, MARCELL, CERRATO, KAZEEM, ROWE, ZIMMERMAN, GREEN AND DALEY, FEBRUARY 11, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON HEALTH, FEBRUARY 11, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating June 23, 2025, as "Widows' and Widowers' Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, The American Community Survey, an annual demographics survey conducted by the United States Census Bureau, estimates that in 2023, the most recent year data is available, 6.3% of this Commonwealth's population, or approximately 687,684 individuals, were widowed; andWHEREAS, Losing a spouse can be one of the most difficult things a person experiences; andWHEREAS, Caring for widows and widowers is an important teaching for several of the world's religions; andWHEREAS, Caring for widows and widowers can include encouraging them to talk to a qualified counselor, maintain their self-care and reach out to family and friends; andWHEREAS, The Federal Government has taken steps toward caring for widows and widowers, including survivor benefits through the Social Security program; andWHEREAS, The Commonwealth has taken steps toward caring for 123456789101112131415161718 widows and widowers, including the Property Tax/Rent Rebate Program; andWHEREAS, June 23 is recognized globally as "International Widows' Day"; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives designate June 23, 2025, as "Widows' and Widowers' Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives express its condolences to the widows and widowers of this Commonwealth; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives encourage the widows and widowers of this Commonwealth to seek support as they heal from their losses, including seeking support, counseling and comfort offered by family and friends.20250HR0070PN0560 - 2 - 1234567891011121314
