2025-2026 Regular Session HR 208 PN 1520 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1520 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.208 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY FLEMING, MARCELL, HANBIDGE, SANCHEZ, KAZEEM, KHAN, BOROWSKI, HOWARD, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, CIRESI, HOHENSTEIN, SCHLOSSBERG, SHUSTERMAN, MALAGARI, KENYATTA, FREEMAN, HILL-EVANS AND GREEN, APRIL 28, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON HUMAN SERVICES, APRIL 28, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDirecting the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee to conduct a study and issue a report on the services, processes and wait times in the Office of Vocational Rehabilitation and the Bureau of Blindness and Visual Services within the Department of Labor and Industry.WHEREAS, The Office of Vocational Rehabilitation and the Bureau of Blindness and Visual Services within the Department of Labor and Industry are obligated to provide specialized, individualized services and orientation and mobility services to the blind and visually impaired population in this Commonwealth; andWHEREAS, The primary mission of the Office of Vocational Rehabilitation is to provide vocational rehabilitation services to assist Pennsylvanians with vision impairment to obtain, maintain and return to employment; andWHEREAS, The mission of the Bureau of Blindness and Visual Services is to provide support services to those with visual impairment; and123456789101112131415161718 WHEREAS, There are concerns that the Office of Vocational Rehabilitation and the Bureau of Blindness and Visual Services do not have adequate personnel to meet their missions in a timely manner; andWHEREAS, Many of the customers of the Office of Vocational Rehabilitation and the Bureau of Blindness and Visual Services experience waiting periods for essential support and services; andWHEREAS, The Department of Labor and Industry, through the Office of Vocational Rehabilitation and the Bureau of Blindness and Visual Services, has a statutory mandate to provide services to this vulnerable population in Article XXII(b) of the act of April 9, 1929 (P.L.177, No.175), known as The Administrative Code of 1929; andWHEREAS, Without these services, many Pennsylvanians may have no choice but to enter long-term care; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives direct the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee to conduct a study and issue a report on the services, processes and wait times in the Office of Vocational Rehabilitation and the Bureau of Blindness and Visual Services within the Department of Labor and Industry; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the study:(1) research the current wait times for customers seeking services and make recommendations on improving wait times;(2) examine the impact on the customers waiting for services and, if possible, determine how an extended wait period impacts the life and potential careers of the customers; and20250HR0208PN1520 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 (3) examine the process by which customers are referred for outside services to provide potential solutions for reducing the waiting list or serving customers more efficiently, including the potential for referral and contracting to an outside agency such as a Statewide nonprofit service provider association for blind and visually impaired residents of this Commonwealth;and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee issue a report of its findings and recommendations to the General Assembly within 180 days of the adoption of this resolution.20250HR0208PN1520 - 3 - 123456789101112
