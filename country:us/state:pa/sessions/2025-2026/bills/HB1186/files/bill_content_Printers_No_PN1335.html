Adjunct Prosecutor Division Restricted Account.

hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. Chapter 87 of Title 42 of the Pennsylvania
Consolidated Statutes is amended by adding a subchapter to read:

8731. Scope of subchapter.
This subchapter relates to the Office of Attorney General.
8732. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this subchapter
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Account." The Adjunct Prosecutor Division Restricted
Account established in this subchapter.
"Adjunct prosecutor." An adjunct prosecutor of the division.
"Attorney General." The Attorney General of the
Commonwealth.
"District attorney." A district attorney in this
Commonwealth.
"Division." The Adjunct Prosecutor Division established in
this subchapter.
8733. Adjunct Prosecutor Division.
The Adjunct Prosecutor Division is established in the Office
of Attorney General. The purpose of the division is to assist
district attorneys with the investigation and prosecution of
criminal matters as provided in this subchapter.
8734. Request for assistance.
A district attorney may request that the division provide an
adjunct prosecutor to assist in the investigation or prosecution
of a criminal matter under the laws of this Commonwealth.

Following receipt of a request under section 8734 (relating
to request for assistance), the division may assign an adjunct
prosecutor to temporarily serve under the direction of the

8737. Scope of authority.
(a) Qualification required.--An adjunct prosecutor must be a
member in good standing of the bar of this Commonwealth.
(b) Powers and duties.--An adjunct prosecutor's
investigative and prosecutorial powers and duties may include,
but not be limited to, with the consent and under the direction
of the district attorney, the following:
(1) Conducting proceedings before grand juries.
(2) Participating in court proceedings and engaging in
litigation, including a civil and criminal matter, that a
requesting district attorney deems necessary.
(3) Reviewing documentary evidence available from any
source.
(4) Making applications to a State court for a grant of
immunity to a witness, consistent with applicable statutory
requirements, or for a warrant, subpoena or other court
order.
(c) Other powers.--
(1) For the purpose of carrying out the duties of the
division, an adjunct prosecutor may use the facilities,
resources and personnel of the Attorney General, including
investigators, attorneys and necessary experts, to assist
with a criminal investigation or prosecution.
(2) An adjunct prosecutor may request assistance from
the Pennsylvania State Police or any law enforcement agency

8738. Standing.
A person charged with a violation of the law by an adjunct
prosecutor has no standing to challenge the authority of the
adjunct prosecutor to prosecute the case. If a person makes a
challenge to the adjunct prosecutor's authority, the challenge
shall be dismissed and no relief shall be available in the
courts of this Commonwealth to the person.
8739. Adjunct Prosecutor Division Restricted Account.
(a) Establishment.--The Adjunct Prosecutor Division
Restricted Account is established in the State Treasury.
(b) Source of funding.--The account shall consist of money
appropriated by the General Assembly to the account and interest
earned on money in the account.
(c) Duty of Attorney General.--The Attorney General shall
submit an annual request for an appropriation from the account
under Article VI of the act of April 9, 1929 (P.L.177, No.175),
known as The Administrative Code of 1929.
(d) Lapsing.--An appropriation from the account shall lapse
to the General Fund in accordance with section 621 of The
Administrative Code of 1929.
Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.
