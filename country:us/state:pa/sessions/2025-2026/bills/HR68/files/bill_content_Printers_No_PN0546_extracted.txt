2025-2026 Regular Session HR 68 PN 0546 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 546 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.68 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY STRUZZI, PICKETT, REICHARD, SAPPEY AND SMITH, FEBRUARY 10, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON TRANSPORTATION, FEBRUARY 10, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDirecting the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee to conduct a study and issue a report on the current status, management and implementation of mass or public transit in Pennsylvania's rural communities.WHEREAS, Access to efficient and reliable transportation is essential for the economic, social and environmental well-being of all communities; andWHEREAS, Rural communities in this Commonwealth often face unique challenges in providing adequate transportation options due to their dispersed populations, limited infrastructure and geographical constraints; andWHEREAS, Both the legislative and the executive branches play a crucial role in shaping the transportation landscape of this Commonwealth and have the responsibility to ensure equitable access to transportation services for all residents, including residents in rural areas; andWHEREAS, Investing in and improving mass or public transit systems in rural communities can enhance mobility, stimulate economic growth, reduce traffic congestion, enhance 12345678910111213141516171819 environmental sustainability and improve overall quality of life; andWHEREAS, A comprehensive study specifically focusing on mass or public transit options in rural communities will provide valuable insights into the transportation needs, challenges and opportunities in these areas and inform future policy and investment decisions; andWHEREAS, By conducting a study on mass or public transit in rural communities, the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee can gather data, evaluate existing transportation services, identify gaps and deficiencies, explore innovative solutions and recommend strategies to improve access to affordable and efficient transit options for rural residents; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives direct the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee to undertake a comprehensive study on mass or public transit in Pennsylvania's rural communities; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the study:(1) Assess the existing transportation infrastructure, including road networks, bridges and mass or public transit services, in rural communities across this Commonwealth.(2) Identify the transportation needs, challenges and priorities of rural residents, considering factors, including employment, education, health care and access to essential services.(3) Evaluate the feasibility and effectiveness of various mass or public transit options, including bus services, ride-sharing programs, on-demand services and innovative solutions using emerging technologies.(4) Examine successful models and best practices 20250HR0068PN0546 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 implemented in other states to address transportation challenges in rural areas.(5) Engage with local communities, stakeholders, leaders, transportation experts and other relevant entities to gather input, feedback and insights throughout the study process.(6) Develop a comprehensive set of recommendations and strategies to enhance mass or public transit options in rural communities, considering cost-effectiveness, environmental impact and long-term sustainability;and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Legislative Budget and Finance Committee prepare a report of its findings and recommendations and submit the report to the House of Representatives no later than 12 months after the adoption of this resolution.20250HR0068PN0546 - 3 - 123456789101112131415
