AN ACT
Amending the act of December 19, 1996 (P.L.1478, No.190),
entitled "An act relating to the recycling and reuse of waste
tires; providing for the proper disposal of waste tires and
the cleanup of stockpiled tires; authorizing investment tax
credits for utilizing waste tires; providing remediation
grants for the cleanup of tire piles and for pollution
prevention programs for small business and households;
establishing the Small Business and Household Pollution
Prevention Program and management standards for small
business hazardous waste; providing for a household hazardous
waste program and for grant programs; making appropriations;
and making repeals," in waste tire recycling, providing for
replacement tires.

Section 106.5. Replacement tires.
(a) Mandatory recycling.--If an individual brings a vehicle
to a business entity that sells tires to replace a waste tire
from the vehicle with a new tire:
(1) The business entity shall retain the waste tire for
the purpose of recycling.
(2) The individual may not remove the waste tire from
the business entity or otherwise retain the waste tire.
(b) Applicability.--
(1) This section shall not apply to a tire that does not
need to be replaced but simply needs to be patched or
otherwise repaired.
(2) This section shall apply to a tire that:
(i) No longer meets safety inspection standards.
(ii) The business entity believes will no longer
meet safety inspection standards within the next three
months.
(c) Definitions.--As used in this section, the following
words and phrases shall have the meanings given to them in this
subsection unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Vehicle." As defined in 75 Pa.C.S. 102 (relating to
definitions).
Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.
