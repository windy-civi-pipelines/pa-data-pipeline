2025-2026 Regular Session HR 243 PN 1778 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1778 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.243 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY ABNEY, BRENNAN, HILL-EVANS, GIRAL, VENKAT, KENYATTA, SANCHEZ, BURGOS, DEASY AND SCHLOSSBERG, MAY 30, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON TOURISM, RECREATION AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT, MAY 30, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDesignating June 9, 2025, as "Dave Parker Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, On June 9, 1951, David Gene Parker was born in Grenada, Mississippi, to Richard and Dannie Mae Parker; andWHEREAS, The Parkers moved to Cincinnati, Ohio, in 1956 where Dave grew up as a standout athlete in football, basketball and baseball, drawing interest from professional and college scouts; andWHEREAS, After a significant knee injury his senior year, the Pittsburgh Pirates drafted Parker in the 14th round of the 1970 amateur draft; andWHEREAS, Parker made his major league debut with the Pittsburgh Pirates on July 12, 1973, beginning his 19-year major league career as a right fielder, the first 11 of which were with the Pittsburgh Pirates; andWHEREAS, Nicknamed "Cobra," Parker was known for his lively presence and was among the major league's best and brightest 12345678910111213141516 stars of the late 1970s; andWHEREAS, A well-rounded athlete with a legendary throwing arm, Parker won the 1978 National League Most Valuable Player Award, three Gold Glove Awards, three Silver Slugger Awards, two National League batting titles and was a seven-time All-Star; andWHEREAS, Parker was a member of two World Series Champion teams, the 1979 Pittsburgh Pirates and the 1989 Oakland Athletics; andWHEREAS, Parker ended his remarkable career with 2,712 hits, 339 home runs, 1,493 runs batted in and a .290 batting average; andWHEREAS, Following his retirement, Parker spent 15 years on the Baseball Writers Association Hall of Fame ballot, but he never received the necessary votes for election; andWHEREAS, On December 8, 2024, Parker was elected to the National Baseball Hall of Fame through the 16-member Classic Baseball Era Committee, receiving votes on 14 of 16 ballots; andWHEREAS, Parker will be officially inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame on July 27, 2025, in Cooperstown, New York; andWHEREAS, To celebrate his election to the prestigious Baseball Hall of Fame and his career in Pittsburgh, the Commonwealth should honor Dave Parker on his 74th birthday; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives designate June 9, 2025, as "Dave Parker Day" in Pennsylvania.20250HR0243PN1778 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223242526
