2025-2026 Regular Session HB 332 PN 0287 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 287 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE BILL No.332 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY BRIGGS, WAXMAN, GIRAL, WEBSTER, HILL-EVANS, PIELLI, KENYATTA, BRENNAN, SANCHEZ, GUENST, ISAACSON, DONAHUE, KHAN, CIRESI, FREEMAN, OTTEN, HOHENSTEIN, CERRATO AND GREEN, JANUARY 27, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION, JANUARY 27, 2025 AN ACTAmending the act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), entitled "An act relating to the public school system, including certain provisions applicable as well to private and parochial schools; amending, revising, consolidating and changing the laws relating thereto," in terms and courses of study, providing for media literacy education.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. The act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), known as the Public School Code of 1949, is amended by adding a section to read:Section 1556. Media Literacy Education.--(a) The Department of Education, in consultation with the State Board of Education, shall:(1) Develop, within six (6) months of the effective date of this section, an age-appropriate model curriculum for students in kindergarten through grade twelve for media literacy instruction. Beginning with the school year that commences after the effective date of this section, a school district shall 12345678910111213141516171819 incorporate into the school curriculum a component regarding media literacy.(2) The curriculum may include, but need not be limited to:(i) developing critical thinking skills;(ii) understanding how media messages shape culture and society;(iii) identifying target-marketing strategies;(iv) naming techniques of persuasion used;(v) recognizing bias and misinformation;(vi) discovering parts of a story that are not being told; and(vii) evaluating media messages based on personal experiences, skills, beliefs and values.(3) The State Board of Education shall make this curriculum available on the State Board of Education's publicly accessible Internet website.(4) The Department of Education shall make available to school districts on the Department of Education's publicly accessible Internet website a list of resources and materials on media literacy and shall ensure that approved media literacy training opportunities are made available for use in professional development programs for teachers.Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.20250HB0332PN0287 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223
