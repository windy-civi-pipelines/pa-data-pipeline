2025-2026 Regular Session SB 459 PN 0390 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 390 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIASENATE BILL No.459 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY COMITTA, KANE, COLLETT, PISCIOTTANO, BARTOLOTTA, SCHWANK, PENNYCUICK, FONTANA, HAYWOOD, SANTARSIERO, COSTA, VOGEL AND MUTH, MARCH 17, 2025 REFERRED TO EDUCATION, MARCH 17, 2025 AN ACTAmending the act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), entitled "An act relating to the public school system, including certain provisions applicable as well to private and parochial schools; amending, revising, consolidating and changing the laws relating thereto," establishing the Statewide Imagination Library Program and the Imagination Library of Pennsylvania Restricted Account; and imposing duties on the Department of Education.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. The act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), known as the Public School Code of 1949, is amended by adding an article to read:ARTICLE XXIII-BSTATEWIDE IMAGINATION LIBRARY PROGRAMSection 2301-B. Definitions.The following words and phrases when used in this article shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:"Account." The Imagination Library of Pennsylvania 1234567891011121314151617181920 Restricted Account established under section 2302-B."Department." The Department of Education of the Commonwealth."Eligible child." A child who resides within this Commonwealth and is under five years of age."Program." The Statewide Imagination Library Program established under section 2303-B."Qualified local entity." Any existing or new local Dolly Parton's Imagination Library program partner.Section 2302-B. Imagination Library of Pennsylvania Restricted Account.(a) Establishment.--The Imagination Library of Pennsylvania Restricted Account is established as a restricted account in the General Fund. Money in the account is appropriated to the department on a continuing basis for the purposes of this article.(b) Funding.--The State Treasurer may accept appropriations, transfers, gifts, grants, donations, legacies or any other revenues, including allowable Federal funds, for deposit into the account.(c) Allocation.--Money from the account shall be allocated to qualified local entities that agree to a dollar-for-dollar match for purposes of the program. The department may waive the dollar-for-dollar match requirement for a qualified local entity on a case-by-case basis to prevent undue financial hardship.(d) Money shall not lapse.--Money remaining in the account at the close of the fiscal year shall not lapse but shall be carried forward to the next fiscal year and be continuously appropriated only for the purposes specified in this article.(e) Interest.--Any interest that accrues in the account 20250SB0459PN0390 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 shall remain in the account.Section 2303-B. Statewide Imagination Library Program.(a) Establishment and purpose.--The Statewide Imagination Library Program is established in the department to promote the development of a comprehensive Statewide initiative to encourage children from birth to five years of age to develop a love of reading and learning.(b) Providing books.--Qualified local entities shall use allocated money to provide one age-appropriate book to each registered child from birth to five years of age in participating counties. Books shall be sent monthly to each child's home at no cost to families.(c) Matching.--In accordance with section 2302-B(c), the department, if money is available, shall match the amount required of local programs participating in the program.Section 2304-B. Duties of department.(a) Administration.--The department shall administer the program and shall coordinate with a nonprofit entity, qualified under 26 U.S.C. ยง 501(c)(3) (relating to exemption from tax on corporations, certain trusts, etc.) and organized solely to promote and encourage reading by the children of this Commonwealth, for the purpose of implementing this article.(b) Oversight.--The department shall provide oversight to the nonprofit entity in subsection (a) to do all of the following:(1) Manage the daily operations of the program.(2) Promote the Statewide development of local Dolly Parton's Imagination Library programs.(3) Advance and strengthen local Dolly Parton's Imagination Library programs.20250SB0459PN0390 - 3 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 (4) Develop community engagement initiatives.(5) Develop, promote and coordinate a public awareness campaign to make donors aware of the opportunity to donate to the affiliate programs and make the public aware of the opportunity to register eligible children to receive books through the program.(6) Administer the local match requirement and coordinate the collection and remittance of local program costs for books and mailing.(7) Develop Statewide marketing and communication plans.(8) Solicit donations, gifts and other funding to financially support local Dolly Parton's Imagination Library programs.(c) Regulations.--The department may promulgate regulations as needed for the administration of the program.Section 2305-B. Report.The department shall prepare and submit to the General Assembly, no later than January 1, 2026, and each January 1 thereafter, a report that includes, but is not limited to, the following:(1) The deposits made to, and expenditures made from, the account.(2) Whether the department waived any local match requirements.(3) How many local programs exist, where the programs are located and which entity or organization serves as the local partner.(4) How many children are enrolled in a program and how many books those children have received.Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.20250SB0459PN0390 - 4 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930
