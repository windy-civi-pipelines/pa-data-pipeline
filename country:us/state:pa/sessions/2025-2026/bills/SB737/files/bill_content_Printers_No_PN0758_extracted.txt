Section 5602 of Title 53 of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes is amended by adding a definition to read:ยง 5602. Definitions.The following words and phrases when used in this chapter shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:* * *"Public safety projects." The term includes any or all of the following:(1) Emergency medical services.(2) Both the provision and the availability of services, including administrative support, coordination of service 123456789101112131415161718 delivery and financing services, relating to emergency medical services.* * *Section 2. Title 53 is amended by adding a section to read:ยง 5606.1. Public safety authorities.(a) Authorization.--Except as provided under subsection (h) , the creation of an authority for the purpose of public safety projects shall be restricted to a county. Counties may enter into an agreement to create a joint authority under this subsection.(b) Municipal representation.--A county that creates a public safety authority shall make a reasonable effort to include representatives of the municipalities the county serves in the governance structure of the public safety authority.(c) Satisfaction of requirement to provide emergency services.--A municipality's requirement under the laws of this Commonwealth to provide emergency services shall be satisfied by entering into a contract or agreement with a public safety authority under this section.(d) Contracts.-- (1) Municipalities may enter into contracts or agreements with the authority as may be deemed necessary or convenient in connection with a public safety project.(2) Except as provided in paragraph (3), a public safety authority may not provide services or assess rates or other charges without the municipality opting by ordinance to enter into a contract with the authority.(3) In the case of an authority operating as a dedicated emergency response organization as defined in 35 Pa.C.S. ยง 7332 (relating to definitions), nothing in this subsection is 20250SB0737PN0758 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 intended to interfere with or relieve the dedicated emergency response organization from an obligation or commitment to respond to a dispatch from a public safety answering point or mutual aid system or to invoice and receive payment from an appropriate party for the services rendered.(e) Rates and other charges.--(1) In addition to any other power of an authority to fix, alter, charge and collect rates and other charges in the area served by its facilities, a public safety authority may fix, alter, charge and collect rates and other charges for the services that the authority provides, which rates and charges shall be reasonable and uniform. The fixing or altering of a rate or other charge or a change to the manner in which a rate or other charge is charged or collected under this subsection must comply with all applicable notice requirements under 65 Pa.C.S. Ch. 7 (relating to open meetings) and 45 Pa.C.S. (relating to legal notices).(2) A public safety authority must comply with section 5612 (relating to money of authority).(f) Existing authorities.--Notwithstanding any provision of this section, an authority that provides public safety services in existence on the effective date of this section shall be permitted to continue operation, without limitation, as a public safety project as provided under this section.(g) Duties of Department of Community and Economic Development.--The Department of Community and Economic Development, in consultation with the Director of the Bureau of Emergency Medical Services of the Department of Health in relation to emergency medical services authorities, shall:(1) Work with relevant stakeholders to develop guidance 20250SB0737PN0758 - 3 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 and assistance for counties to create public safety authorities under this section.(2) Contract with educational and technical assistance providers to aid counties in starting and operating public safety authorities.(h) Municipal authorization.--Municipalities in a county may create a joint public safety authority if:(1) the county, by resolution, states that it does not intend to create a public safety authority; or(2) municipalities in the county petition the county, by passing resolutions, to create a public safety authority and the county does not create the authority within 120 days of receiving the petition.Section 3.
