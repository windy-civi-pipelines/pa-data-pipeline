2025-2026 Regular Session SR 134 PN 1095 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1095 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIASENATE RESOLUTION No.134 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY BROOKS, STEFANO, KEEFER, TARTAGLIONE, J. WARD AND VOGEL, JULY 31, 2025 REFERRED TO CONSUMER PROTECTION AND PROFESSIONAL LICENSURE, JULY 31, 2025 A RESOLUTIONDirecting the Joint State Government Commission to conduct a study and provide an analysis on the implementation and effects of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact Act, Psychology Interjurisdictional Compact Act, Physical Therapy Licensure Compact Act and the Nurse Licensure Compact Act, and the effects of interstate medical licensure compacts nationwide on their respective states and this Commonwealth.WHEREAS, In this Commonwealth, legislation has been enacted to join the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact, Psychology Interjurisdictional Compact, Physical Therapy Licensure Compact and Nurse Licensure Compact; andWHEREAS, Legislation has been introduced in the General Assembly to join the Social Work Licensure Compact, Counseling Compact, Interstate Dental and Dental Hygiene Licensure Compact and National Occupational Therapy Licensure Compact; andWHEREAS, There is potential for legislation to be introduced in the General Assembly to join the Respiratory Care Interstate Compact; andWHEREAS, Such compacts are intended to enable health care providers to more easily practice their licensed area in other 1234567891011121314151617181920 states and provide increased access to these providers for Pennsylvania residents; andWHEREAS, As further compacts are proposed, approved and implemented, it is important to understand the impacts they have on their respective state and surrounding states; andWHEREAS, The intent of this resolution is for the Joint State Government Commission to further collect data so policies can be reviewed to ensure that interstate medical licensure compacts enacted in this Commonwealth are improving the access to care and quality medical providers; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the Senate direct the Joint State Government Commission to conduct a study and provide an analysis on the implementation and effects of the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact Act, Psychology Interjurisdictional Compact Act, Physical Therapy Licensure Compact Act and the Nurse Licensure Compact Act, and the effects of interstate medical licensure compacts nationwide on their respective states and this Commonwealth; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the study include information and analysis of each interstate medical licensure compact previously mentioned within this resolution, as well as any further interstate medical licensure compacts that the study may identify, including all of the following:(1) Whether the compact is improving access to health care.(2) The percentage of time a licensed health care provider is practicing in the licensed health care provider's home state versus other states as allowed by the compact.(3) The impact the compact has had on stopping the hemorrhage of health care professionals from the health care 20250SR0134PN1095 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 professional's respective state.(4) Whether the compact is bringing more providers into the state to practice, or if more providers are leaving the state to practice.(5) Whether applications for licenses are being processed more expeditiously than prior to the enactment of the compact, and a comparison of licensing times per state.(6) Whether health care providers are utilizing the implementation of the compact, and reasons they may not be.(7) Compact license fees per state.(8) How Pennsylvania residents are protected from health care providers that are practicing in this Commonwealth due to the implementation of a compact that have had a complaint filed against the health care providers in another state.(9) Changes in a state licensing board's authority to control and pertinent information from individual state licensing boards;and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Joint State Government Commission issue a report of its findings, along with any statutory or
