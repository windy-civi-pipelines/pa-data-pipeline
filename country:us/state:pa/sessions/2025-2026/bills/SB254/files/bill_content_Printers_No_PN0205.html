AN ACT
Amending the act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), entitled "An
act relating to the public school system, including certain
provisions applicable as well to private and parochial
schools; amending, revising, consolidating and changing the
laws relating thereto," providing for the STEM Grant Program;
and making an appropriation.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. The act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), known
as the Public School Code of 1949, is amended by adding an
article to read:
ARTICLE XV-O
STEM GRANT PROGRAM
Section 1501-O. Scope of article.
This article relates to the STEM Grant Program.
Section 1502-O. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this article
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:

"Nonpublic school." A nonprofit organization, charter
school, cyber charter school, private school or a nonprofit
school, other than a public school, in this Commonwealth where a
resident of this Commonwealth may legally fulfill the compulsory
school attendance requirements of this act and which meets the
requirements of 42 U.S.C. Ch. 21 Subch. V (relating to federally

"Secretary." The Secretary of Education of the Commonwealth.
"STEM." Science, technology, engineering and mathematics.
"STEM subject." A class for which the instruction relates to
the topics of science, technology, engineering or mathematics.
Section 1503-O. Administrations.
(a) Selection of administrator.--Within 30 days of the
effective date of this subsection, the department shall issue a
request for applications for an intermediate unit to administer
the program.
(b) Applications.--The selected administrator shall develop
an application process for the program consistent with the
provisions of section 1504-O(a).
(c) Notification.--The selected administrator shall annually
notify all educators employed by the school districts or
nonpublic schools within this Commonwealth of the program and
shall submit a list of all eligible educators who express
interest in participating in the program to the department by
January 15 of each year. The secretary shall post the list on
the department's publicly accessible Internet website by January
30 of each year.

Section 1504-O. Application and acceptance.
(a) Application.--A nonpublic school that elects to
participate in the program shall submit an application to the
secretary in a manner and on a form as determined by the
secretary. The secretary shall establish the application
deadlines for each school year. An application shall include the
following:
(1) The names of the public and nonpublic school and
eligible educators from each that shall form a STEM
partnership under the program.
(2) A statement from the nonpublic school and the
eligible educator that the eligible educator shall teach at
the nonpublic school during hours when the educator has no
conflicting public school duties.
(3) The proposed schedule and grades to be taught by the
eligible educator at the nonpublic school.
(4) The number of students, based on enrollment
projections, who will receive instruction from the eligible
educator under the proposed schedule at the nonpublic school.
(5) A certification from the nonpublic school that the
eligible educator shall provide only secular instruction at
the nonpublic school.
(6) Any other information specified by the secretary.
(b) Notification.--Upon receiving an application under
subsection (a), the secretary shall, within 10 business days of

Section 1505-O. Grant awards.
(a) Award.--The secretary shall award grants under the
program based on review of the applications and subject to the
limit of money appropriated or otherwise made available for the
program. Grant money for approved applications shall be
allocated by the department to the selected administrator.
(b) Use of funds.--The selected administrator shall use the
funds to provide compensation to a participating nonpublic
school as reimbursement for costs incurred during the previous
school year for the services of an eligible educator under the
program. The secretary shall establish the amount of each grant
awarded to a participating nonpublic school using the following
calculation:
(1) An eligible educator shall receive an amount equal
to the educator's hourly wage multiplied by the number of
hours the educator teaches STEM subjects at a nonpublic
school under the program multiplied by a factor of 1.25 to
account for preparatory time.
(2) In the event that sufficient money is not
appropriated to fully fund the compensation to be provided to
eligible educators under paragraph (1), the secretary shall
apportion the amount appropriated for the implementation of
this article among the eligible educators in proportion to
the compensation each participating eligible educator would
have received had the full amount been appropriated.
