2025-2026 Regular Session HR 188 PN 1373 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1373 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.188 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY MATZIE, RIVERA, MAYES, VENKAT, MADDEN, KAZEEM, McNEILL, GIRAL, FREEMAN, HANBIDGE, BRENNAN, NEILSON, KHAN, HILL-EVANS, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, HOWARD, SANCHEZ, CERRATO, GUZMAN, SCHLOSSBERG, RUSNOCK, O'MARA, CONKLIN AND CIRESI, APRIL 15, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON HEALTH, APRIL 15, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing May 6, 2025, as "World Asthma Day" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, The National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute defines asthma as a chronic lung disease that inflames and narrows the airways, resulting in recurring periods of wheezing, chest tightness, shortness of breath and chronic coughing; andWHEREAS, Approximately 25 million, or 1 in 12, individuals in the United States are known to have asthma; andWHEREAS, The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reported that an estimated 1 million adults and an estimated 300,000 children in Pennsylvania suffer from asthma; andWHEREAS, There is no known cure for asthma, but once asthma is properly diagnosed and a treatment plan is implemented, individuals are able to better manage symptoms and improve their quality of life; andWHEREAS, In an effort to raise awareness about asthma and improve asthma care throughout the world, health care groups, 12345678910111213141516 asthma educators, patients and advocates annually celebrate the first Tuesday of May as "World Asthma Day"; andWHEREAS, This day encourages individuals to focus on daily management and ongoing education of asthma and to identify allergens and irritants that can lead to and worsen asthma attacks; andWHEREAS, Individuals who participate in "World Asthma Day" use this occasion as an opportunity to organize and participate in local events, screenings and group discussions to become more knowledgeable about asthma control; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize May 6, 2025, as "World Asthma Day" in Pennsylvania; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives encourage all residents of this Commonwealth to work together to help all Pennsylvanians living with asthma control their symptoms.20250HR0188PN1373 - 2 - 123456789101112131415
