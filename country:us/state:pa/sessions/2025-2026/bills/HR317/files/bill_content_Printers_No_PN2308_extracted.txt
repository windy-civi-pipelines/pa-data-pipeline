2025-2026 Regular Session HR 317 PN 2308 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 2308 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.317 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY KINKEAD, PASHINSKI, FREEMAN, HILL-EVANS, WAXMAN, McNEILL, HOWARD, PROBST, CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, VITALI, ISAACSON, MADDEN, GIRAL, SANCHEZ, VENKAT, STEELE, SAMUELSON, KHAN, DEASY, SCHLOSSBERG, NEILSON, HOHENSTEIN, CERRATO, MALAGARI, CIRESI, O'MARA, MENTZER AND GREEN, SEPTEMBER 12, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON AGRICULTURE AND RURAL AFFAIRS, SEPTEMBER 15, 2025 A RESOLUTIONRecognizing the month of September 2025 as "Hunger Action Month" in Pennsylvania.WHEREAS, Hunger and food insecurity are issues of vital concern in Pennsylvania where, according to Feeding America, more than 1.5 million individuals experience food insecurity and one in every six children do not know where their next meal will come from; andWHEREAS, Everyone needs nutritious food to thrive and in every community in America people are working hard to provide for themselves and their families yet 47 million people, including more than 14 million children, face food insecurity in the United States; andWHEREAS, Food insecurity, the condition of limited or uncertain access to adequate food, is associated with numerous adverse social and health outcomes and is a critical public health issue; and12345678910111213141516 WHEREAS, The fight against hunger requires engaging a wide range of public and private entities, including policymakers, business leaders, food banks, community organizations, farmers, producers and concerned residents; andWHEREAS, The Commonwealth is committed to taking action to combat hunger in every corner of our State and to provide additional resources to those in need; andWHEREAS, The Commonwealth is working with Feeding Pennsylvania and Hunger-Free Pennsylvania to educate people about the role and importance of food banks in addressing hunger and raise awareness about the need to devote more resources and attention to hunger issues; andWHEREAS, Feeding Pennsylvania, Hunger-Free Pennsylvania and antihunger organizations across this Commonwealth distributed more than 164 million pounds of food last year to Pennsylvanians experiencing food insecurity; andWHEREAS, The month of September is recognized as "Hunger Action Month" across the country in order to bring attention to the issue of food insecurity in our communities and to mobilize the public in the movement to end hunger; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives recognize the month of September 2025 as "Hunger Action Month" in Pennsylvania.20250HR0317PN2308 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617181920212223
