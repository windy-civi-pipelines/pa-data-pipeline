AN ACT
Amending Title 75 (Vehicles) of the Pennsylvania Consolidated
Statutes, in general provisions, further providing for
definitions; in licensing of drivers, further providing for
examination of applicant for driver's license; and, in
special vehicles and pedestrians, providing for low-speed
electric scooters.
This act may be referred to as Abby's Law.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. Section 102 of Title 75 of the Pennsylvania
Consolidated Statutes is amended by adding a definition to read:
§ 102. Definitions.
Subject to additional definitions contained in subsequent
provisions of this title which are applicable to specific
provisions of this title, the following words and phrases when
used in this title shall have, unless the context clearly
indicates otherwise, the meanings given to them in this section:
* * *
"Low-speed electric scooter." As follows:
(1) A device weighing less than 100 pounds that:

§ 3597.1. Scope of subchapter.
This subchapter relates to the operation of low-speed
electric scooters on certain highways or roadways in this
Commonwealth.
§ 3597.2. Title and registration exemption.
Notwithstanding Subchapter A of Chapter 11 (relating to
certificate of title) or Subchapter A of Chapter 13 (relating to
general provisions), a low-speed electric scooter authorized
under this section shall not be required to satisfy certificate
of title or vehicle registration requirements under this title.
§ 3597.3. Operation of low-speed electric scooters.
(a) Speed limitation.--A person may not operate a low-speed
electric scooter at a speed greater than 20 miles per hour on a
pedalcycle lane on a highway, a roadway or a pedalcycle path.
(b) Age limitation.--A person under 16 years of age may not
operate a low-speed electric scooter.
(c) Helmet requirement.--A person under 18 years of age may
not operate a low-speed electric scooter unless the person is
wearing a pedalcycle helmet that meets the requirements of
section 3510 (relating to pedalcycle helmets for certain
persons).
(d) Number of riders.--A low-speed electric scooter may not
be operated to carry more persons at one time than the number
for which the device is designed and equipped.
§ 3597.4. Applicability of pedalcycle traffic laws.
(a) General rule.--A person riding a low-speed electric
scooter upon a roadway has all rights and is subject to all
duties applicable to a rider of a pedalcycle under this title,

the Pedalcycle and Pedestrian Advisory Committee established
under Subchapter D (relating to Pedalcycle and Pedestrian

communication and education materials for municipalities and
local law enforcement to use for the education of riders and
drivers on the law relating to pedestrians and pedalcycles,
including low-speed electric scooters. The department shall post
the educational resources on the department's publicly
accessible Internet website.

§ 3597.6. Local enforcement.
A city, borough, incorporated town, township or home rule
municipality that has its own police department may pass an
ordinance to allow for enhanced penalties, for a violation of
section 3597.3(b) (relating to operation of low-speed electric
scooters). The ordinance may create fines for a first offense,
second offense and subsequent offenses but a single fine may not
exceed $250.

(3) The remainder of this act shall take effect in 90
days.
