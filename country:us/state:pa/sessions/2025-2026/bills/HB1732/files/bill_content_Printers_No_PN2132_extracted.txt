Section 7(b)(1) and (6) of the act of January 28, 1988 (P.L.24, No.11), known as the Private Academic Schools Act, are amended to read:Section 7. Application for license.* * *(b) Application contents.--The application shall require a statement showing:(1) The title or name and proposed address of the school, grades and classes, together with the name of the owners and controlling officers. Issuance and renewal of license.* * *(a.1) Conditional approval of licensure.-- (1) The board may grant conditional approval of initial licensure, which approval is provisional and temporary and does not exceed six months, if the board finds that the application and the school for which a license is sought substantially complies with this act and the rules and regulations promulgated under this act but certain application components remain pending, which may include:(i) formal hiring of staffing projections identified in the application or otherwise properly qualified staff; and(ii) submission of an occupying lease or face copy of the deed and facility inspection completed by the department as provided for in section 12(a).20250HB1732PN2132 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 (2) The board may not grant an initial license under paragraph (1) to an applicant school until the applicant school submits evidence fulfilling the conditions for approval. Section 14 of the act is amended to read:Section 14. Promulgation of rules and regulations.(a) Rules and regulations generally.--The board shall promulgate rules and regulations necessary to carry out the purposes of this act. The rules and regulations of the State Board of Private Academic Schools in force on the effective date of this act, and not countermanded by this act, shall remain in effect until repealed or amended by the board[, but not for a period of more than one year].(b) Qualifications for teachers, professional staff and directors .-- (1) Not later than 180 days after the effective date of this paragraph, the board shall promulgate rules and regulations and establish policies, principles and standards necessary to carry out the purposes of this act as they relate to the qualifications of teachers, professional staff and directors, which policies:(i) Provide the authority for private academic schools to apply for emergency permits and long-term or day-to-day substitute permits through the department .
