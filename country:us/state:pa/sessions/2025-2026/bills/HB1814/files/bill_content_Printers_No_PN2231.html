AN ACT
Amending the act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), entitled "An
act relating to the public school system, including certain
provisions applicable as well to private and parochial
schools; amending, revising, consolidating and changing the
laws relating thereto," in pupils and attendance, providing
for possession and use of cell phones.
The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. The act of March 10, 1949 (P.L.30, No.14), known
as the Public School Code of 1949, is amended by adding a
section to read:
Section 1317.4. Possession and Use of Cell Phones.--(a)
Except as provided in this section, a student of a public school
entity may not possess or use a cell phone during the school day
in a school building of the public school entity.
(b) Upon entering the school building at the beginning of
the school day, the student shall:
(1) deposit the cell phone in a lockbox or other secure
location for the duration of the school day; or
(2) secure the cell phone in a case or pouch with a locking

mechanism that prohibits the student from accessing the cell

of being carried by the student.
(c) At the end of the school day, the student may:
(1) retrieve the cell phone from the lockbox or other secure
location; or
(2) unlock the case or pouch described in subsection (b)(2).
(d) This section does not apply to a student if:
(1) the student:
(i) is a member of a volunteer fire company, ambulance or
rescue squad; or
(ii) has a need for the cell phone due to the medical
condition of an immediate family member; and
(2) an administrator of the public school entity approves
possession and use of the cell phone by the student during the
school day in a school building of the public school entity.
(e) A public school entity shall develop or update its
written policies to implement this section. The written policies
shall be subject to the approval of the Department of Education.
(f) As used in this section, the term "public school entity"
means a school district, independent school, area career and
technical school, intermediate unit, charter school or regional
charter school .
Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.
