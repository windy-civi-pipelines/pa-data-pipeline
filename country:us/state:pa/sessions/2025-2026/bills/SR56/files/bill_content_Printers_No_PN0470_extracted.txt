2025-2026 Regular Session SR 56 PN 0470 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 470 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIASENATE RESOLUTION No.56 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY J. WARD, FARRY, PHILLIPS-HILL, FONTANA, LAUGHLIN, HAYWOOD, MARTIN, HUTCHINSON, ARGALL, BROWN, COMITTA, TARTAGLIONE, LANGERHOLC, PITTMAN, ROTHMAN, PENNYCUICK, SANTARSIERO, ROBINSON, CULVER, BAKER, PICOZZI AND BROOKS, MARCH 24, 2025 INTRODUCED AND ADOPTED, MARCH 24, 2025 A RESOLUTIONHonoring the life and extending condolences to the family of the Honorable Jerry Allen Stern.WHEREAS, The Honorable Jerry Allen Stern, a former member of the House of Representatives, passed away on December 11, 2024, at 69 years of age; andWHEREAS, Born in Roaring Spring on January 11, 1955, Mr. Stern was the son of the late Merle C. and Vera M. Replogle Stern; andWHEREAS, A 1972 graduate of Central High School, Mr. Stern attended the Altoona School of Commerce, Penn State Altoona and the Altoona Bible Institute; andWHEREAS, Formerly employed with the Feather Petroleum Company and Wineland's IGA Foodliner in Martinsburg, Mr. Stern became Deputy Prothonotary for Blair County in 1982 and Prothonotary and Clerk of Courts in 1989; andWHEREAS, Elected to the House of Representatives of 12345678910111213141516 Pennsylvania in 1992, Mr. Stern served the 80th Legislative District until 2015 and during his tenure, he served as Minority Caucus Secretary, chair of the Tourism and Recreational Development Committee and a member of the Pennsylvania Commission on Crime and Delinquency, the Task Force on Commonwealth-Owned Lands and the Legislative Data Processing Center Commission; andWHEREAS, Instrumental in obtaining funding to expand the Greenfield Township sewage treatment plant and to benefit the Spring Cove School District and five local libraries, Mr. Stern supported the Blair County Convention Center and water projects that benefited agriculture in the Susquehanna River basin; andWHEREAS, A lifetime member of the Martinsburg Grace Brethren Church, Mr. Stern was a lay minister for many local churches, and a member of the local Rotary Club, serving as club president and as assistant governor for the district; andWHEREAS, Mr. Stern was active with numerous organizations, including the Blair County Chamber of Commerce, the Blair County Farm Bureau, Nason Hospital, the Martinsburg Sportsmen's Association, the Hollidaysburg Veterans' Home Advisory Council, the Hollidaysburg American Legion Ambulance Service Board and American Cancer Society, Inc.; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the Senate honor the life and extend condolences to the family of the Honorable Jerry Allen Stern; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Senate recognize Mr. Stern's life which greatly benefited all those who lived, served and worked with him; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the Senate extend heartfelt condolences to his wife of 46 years, Susan Elaine Waite Stern; children, Gretchen 20250SR0056PN0470 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 Bettwy and Jason; five grandchildren; and many other family members and friends.20250SR0056PN0470 - 3 - 12
