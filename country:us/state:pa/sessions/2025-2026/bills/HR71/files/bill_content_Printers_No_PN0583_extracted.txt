2025-2026 Regular Session HR 71 PN 0583 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 583 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.71 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY WEBSTER, VENKAT, KHAN, SANCHEZ, WAXMAN AND HADDOCK, FEBRUARY 12, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON STATE GOVERNMENT, FEBRUARY 12, 2025 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTIONEstablishing the Pennsylvania State Flag Commission to study the history of the State flag of the Commonwealth, solicit design submissions for a new State flag and recommend changes to the State flag.WHEREAS, The act of June 13, 1907 (P.L.560, No.373), entitled "An act designating the official flag of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, and describing the same; providing for the carrying of such flag by the regiments of the National Guard of Pennsylvania; authorizing the Secretary of the Commonwealth to provide, and have deposited in the office of Secretary of the Commonwealth, a model of said flag, and making an appropriation therefor," designates the official flag of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania and provides for the design of the flag; andWHEREAS, The State flag is required to have a blue background, using the same blue found in the United States flag, with the Pennsylvania State coat of arms in the center and edges trimmed with a fringe of knotted yellow silk; andWHEREAS, Pennsylvania is one of many states with a flag that has a seal on a blue background, making it indistinguishable 12345678910111213141516171819 from other state flags; andWHEREAS, The North American Vexillological Association outlined five principles of a good flag, which are to:(1) keep it simple;(2) use meaningful symbolism;(3) use two or three basic colors;(4) avoid lettering or seals; and(5) be distinctive or be related;andWHEREAS, The State flag of the Commonwealth violates every single good flag principle; andWHEREAS, Pennsylvania deserves a State flag that is recognizable and stands out from other states while utilizing good flag principles; therefore be itRESOLVED (the Senate concurring), That the General Assembly establish the Pennsylvania State Flag Commission to study the history of the State flag of the Commonwealth, solicit design submissions for a new State flag and recommend changes to the State flag; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission consist of the following members:(1) one member appointed by the Majority Leader of the Senate;(2) one member appointed by the Minority Leader of the Senate;(3) one member appointed by the Majority Leader of the House of Representatives;(4) one member appointed by the Minority Leader of the House of Representatives;(5) one representative from the Pennsylvania Council on 20250HR0071PN0583 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 the Arts;(6) one representative from the North American Vexillological Association who, at the time of the formation of the commission, resides in this Commonwealth; and(7) three members of the public appointed by the Governor;and be it furtherRESOLVED, That at its first meeting, the commission elect one member to serve as the chairperson of the commission; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission have the authority to add additional members to the commission and create subgroups within the commission as it sees fit; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission seek comments and submissions from the public on recommendations for a new State flag; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the commission choose a flag design, or flag designs, to recommend to the General Assembly to become the new State flag; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the members of the commission serve without compensation but be reimbursed for all necessary and reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of their duties; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That reasonable expenses incurred by the commission be equally divided between the Senate and the House of Representatives; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That the approval of the President pro tempore of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives be required to preauthorize all proposed actions of the commission that will entail the expenditure of State funds and approve all 20250HR0071PN0583 - 3 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 expenditures, including expense reimbursement requests of commission members, prior to payment; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That nothing in this concurrent resolution preclude the commission from consulting or seeking the expertise of any individual, nonprofit entity, Commonwealth agency or office or legislative committee of the General Assembly to aid with the selection of a new State flag;
