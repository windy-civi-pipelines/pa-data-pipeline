56A21. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this subchapter
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Department." The Department of Health of the Commonwealth.
"Medication-induced movement disorders." A range of
neurological conditions characterized by involuntary abnormal
movements associated with prolonged use of certain medications
that are commonly used for the treatment of mental health
conditions.
"Program." The Medication-Induced Movement Disorders
Screening Education Program established under section 56A22(a)
(relating to Medication-Induced Movement Disorders Screening
Education Program).
56A22. Medication-Induced Movement Disorders Screening
Education Program.
(a) Establishment.--The Medication-Induced Movement
Disorders Screening Education Program is established in the
department.
(b) Development.--The department shall develop the program
for residents of this Commonwealth in consultation with relevant
agencies, including:
(1) The Office of Developmental Programs under the
Department of Human Services.
(2) The Office of Mental Health and Substance Abuse

(c) Components.--The program must include:
(1) Educational information on the importance of
screening for and identifying symptoms of medication-induced
movement disorders associated with prolonged use of certain
medications that are commonly used for the treatment of
mental health conditions.
(2) The development of guidance and educational
materials for health care providers on clinical standards and
best practices for screening and treating medication-induced
movement disorders, including for services provided through
telehealth. The guidance shall be:
(i) Informed by the American Psychiatric Association
and other relevant and current professional society
practice guidelines.
(ii) Developed in coordination with relevant health
care provider groups.
(3) Education and outreach on the elimination of stigma
for individuals living with medication-induced movement
disorders related to the treatment of mental health
conditions, including medication-induced movement disorder
awareness materials for public safety officers.

shall develop electronic informational materials and make
available to the public and to health care providers information
regarding symptoms of, screenings for and treatment of
