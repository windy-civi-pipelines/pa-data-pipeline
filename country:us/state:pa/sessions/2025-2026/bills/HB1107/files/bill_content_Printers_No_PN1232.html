The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania
hereby enacts as follows:
Section 1. Short title.
This act shall be known and may be cited as the Adult-use
Cannabis Program Act.
Section 2. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this act shall
have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Adult-use cannabis." Cannabis legalized in this
Commonwealth for personal use by an individual who is 21 years
of age or older.
"Department." The Department of Health of the Commonwealth.
"Farmer-grower." A person, including an individual,
corporation, partnership, association, trust or other entity, or
combination of persons, holding a farmer-grower permit from the
department under this act.
"Grower/processor." As defined under the act of April 17,
2016 (P.L.84, No.16), known as the Medical Marijuana Act.
Section 3. Program.
(a) Establishment.--The Adult-use Cannabis Program is
established within the department.
(b) Administration and enforcement.--The department shall
administer and enforce the program. In doing so, the department
shall develop enforcement procedures, including announced and

Section 4. Farmer-growers generally.
(a) Authorization.--Subject to subsection (b), a farmer-
grower may obtain seed and plant material from a
grower/processor within this Commonwealth to grow, process or
dispense adult-use cannabis in accordance with department
regulations.
(b) Limitations.--A farmer-grower may only grow, store or
harvest adult-use cannabis in an area that:
(1) is delineated to a single site at the address
identified on the permit issued by the department;
(2) does not exceed 10 acres of land;
(3) is enclosed by a roof and walls on all sides;
(4) includes locking systems and other security and
surveillance features required by the department; and
(5) is located within this Commonwealth.
Section 5. Unlawful use of adult-use cannabis.
A person may not grow adult-use cannabis unless the person
has received a farmer-grower permit from the department under
this act.

to grow, process or dispense adult-use cannabis shall:
(1) Be in a form and manner prescribed by the
department.
(2) Include verification of all principals, operators,
financial backers or employees of the person applying for the
farmer-grower permit.

(c) Review and determination.--The department shall review

(d) Considerations.--In determining whether to grant a

department.
Section 7. Duties of farmer-growers.
A farmer-grower, including a person applying for a farmer-
grower permit, has a continuing duty to:
(1) Report to the department any change in facts or
circumstances reflected in the application for the farmer-
grower permit or any newly discovered or occurring fact or
circumstance which is required to be included in the
application, including a change in control of the adult-use
cannabis organization.

Section 8. Fees.
(a) Initial application fee.--A person applying for a
farmer-grower permit shall pay an initial application fee in the
amount of $2,500. The fee is nonrefundable.
(b) Permit fee and period.--A person applying for a farmer-
grower permit shall pay a farmer-grower permit fee in the amount
of $2,500. The following apply:
(1) The farmer-grower permit shall be valid for one
year.
(2) The person shall submit the farmer-grower permit fee
at the time of submission of the application for the farmer-
grower permit.
(3) The fee under this subsection shall be returned if
the department does not grant the farmer-grower permit.
(c) Renewal fee and application.--If a farmer-grower chooses
to renew a farmer-grower permit, the farmer-grower must pay a
renewal fee in the amount of $2,500, which shall cover renewal
for all locations. The following apply:
(1) The renewal fee shall be returned if the department
does not grant the renewal of the farmer-grower permit.
(2) An application to renew a farmer-grower permit must
be filed with the department not more than six months nor
fewer than four months prior to the expiration of the farmer-
grower permit.

Section 9. Capital.
Before issuing an initial farmer-grower permit under this
act, the department shall verify that the person applying for
the farmer-grower permit has enough capital, as determined by
the department, which must be on deposit with a financial
institution.
Section 10. Limitations on farmer-growers and permits.
The following limitations apply:
(1) The department shall promulgate regulations
initially limiting the number of farmer-grower permits
granted under this act. The number of farmer-grower permits
issued shall be determined by the department.
(2) The department may not issue more than one farmer-
grower permit to one person.
(3) A farmer-grower may only provide adult-use cannabis
to a grower/processor holding a valid permit under the act of
April 17, 2016 (P.L.84, No.16), known as the Medical
Marijuana Act.
Section 11. Inventory tracking system.
A farmer-grower must implement an inventory tracking system,
which shall be accessible to the department. The inventory
tracking system shall include tracking of all of the following:
(1) The number of adult-use cannabis plants located on a
site used for growing or cultivating adult-use cannabis.
(2) The number of adult-use cannabis plants located on a
site used for growing or cultivating adult-use cannabis that
have been successfully cultivated for sale to a
grower/processor.

Section 12. Requirements regarding sites.
Sites used for growing or cultivating adult-use cannabis must
be certified, inspected and permitted by and registered with the
department. The failure to permit inspection by the department
shall be a violation of this act.
Section 13. Reports.
Within one year of the issuance of the first permit to a
farmer-grower and every three months thereafter in a form and
manner prescribed by the department, the following information
shall be provided to the department, which shall compile the
information and post it on the department's publicly accessible
Internet website:
(1) The amount of adult-use cannabis sold by the farmer-
grower during each three-month period.
(2) The price charged for each amount of adult-use
cannabis sold by the farmer-grower, as determined by the
department, and total amount of revenue received from the
sales.

The department shall develop regulations relating to the
storage, transportation and tracking of adult-use cannabis among
farmer-growers and of medical marijuana to ensure adequate
security to guard against in-transit losses. The regulations
shall provide for transporting adult-use cannabis to a farmer-

Section 15. Tax on adult-use cannabis.
(a) Tax imposed.--A tax is imposed on the gross receipts of
a grower/processor received from the sale of adult-use cannabis
by a farmer-grower. The tax shall be at a rate of 1%. The tax
shall be charged against and be paid by the grower/processor and
shall not be added as a separate charge or line item on any
sales slip, invoice, receipt or other statement or memorandum of
the price paid by a dispensary, patient or caregiver.
(b) Information.--A farmer-grower that sells adult-use
cannabis shall provide to the Department of Revenue information
required by the Department of Revenue.
Section 16. Duties of advisory board.
The advisory board established under section 1201 of the act
of April 17, 2016 (P.L.84, No.16), known as the Medical
Marijuana Act, shall include recommendations and findings as to
whether to change, add or reduce the number of farmer-growers.
Section 17. Zoning.
A farmer-grower shall meet the same municipal zoning and land
use requirements as other agricultural operations that are
located in the same zoning district.
