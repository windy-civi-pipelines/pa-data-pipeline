PRELIMINARY PROVISIONS
Section 101. Short title.
This act shall be known and may be cited as the Advisory
Council on PANDAS and PANS Act.
CHAPTER 2
ADVISORY COUNCIL ON PANDAS AND PANS
Section 201. Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this chapter
shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
context clearly indicates otherwise:
"Advisory council." The Advisory Council on PANDAS and PANS.
"PANDAS." Pediatric Autoimmune Neuropsychiatric Disorders
Associated with Streptococcal Infections.
"PANS." Pediatric Acute-onset Neuropsychiatric Syndrome.
"Secretary." The Secretary of Health of the Commonwealth.
Section 202. Establishment of advisory council.
(a) Establishment.--The secretary shall establish the
Advisory Council on PANDAS and PANS for the purpose of advising
the secretary on PANDAS and PANS, including on matters relating
to the research, diagnosis, treatment and education of PANDAS
and PANS.
(b) Appointed members.--The secretary shall appoint the
following members to the advisory council:
(1) A licensed immunologist who practices in this
Commonwealth and has experience treating individuals with
PANDAS and PANS and the use of intravenous immunoglobulin.
(2) A licensed health care practitioner who practices in
this Commonwealth and has expertise in treating individuals
with PANDAS, PANS, autism spectrum disorder, obsessive-
compulsive disorder and other neurological disorders.

(c) Additional members.--The following shall be members of

Section 203. Compensation of advisory council members.
A member of the advisory council shall receive no
compensation except for reimbursement for necessary expenses
incurred in performing the member's duties.

Section 205. Quorum.
A majority of the advisory council members shall constitute a
quorum. A majority vote of a quorum shall be required for any
official action of the advisory council.
Section 206. Duties of advisory council.
The advisory council shall have the following duties:
(1) The advisory council shall elect the chair of the
advisory council.
(2) No later than January 1, 2026, and annually
thereafter, the advisory council shall submit a report to the
Health and Human Services Committee of the Senate and the
Health Committee of the House of Representatives. The report
shall include, but not be limited to, recommendations on the
following matters:
(i) Practice guidelines for the diagnosis and
treatment of PANDAS and PANS.
(ii) Mechanisms to increase clinical awareness and
education of PANDAS and PANS among health care
practitioners, including pediatricians, school-based
health centers and providers of mental health services.
(iii) Outreach programs for educators and parents to
increase awareness of PANDAS and PANS.
(iv) The development of a network of volunteer
experts on the diagnosis and treatment of PANDAS and PANS
to assist in education and outreach.
