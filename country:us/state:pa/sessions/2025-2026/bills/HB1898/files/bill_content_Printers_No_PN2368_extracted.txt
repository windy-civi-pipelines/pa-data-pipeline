MACKENZIE, SIEGEL, GAYDOS, SANCHEZ, CERRATO, RADER AND FRIEL, SEPTEMBER 30, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON STATE GOVERNMENT, SEPTEMBER 30, 2025 AN ACTAmending the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), entitled "An act concerning elections, including general, municipal, special and primary elections, the nomination of candidates, primary and election expenses and election contests; creating and defining membership of county boards of elections; imposing duties upon the Secretary of the Commonwealth of the Commonwealth, courts, county boards of elections, county commissioners; imposing penalties for violation of the act, and codifying, revising and consolidating the laws relating thereto; and repealing certain acts and parts of acts relating to elections," in nomination of candidates, further providing for number of signers required for nomination petitions of candidates at primaries.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. Section 912.1(13) and (14) of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, are amended to read:Section 912.1. Number of Signers Required for Nomination Petitions of Candidates at Primaries.--Candidates for nomination of offices as listed below shall present a nominating petition containing at least as many valid signatures of registered and enrolled members of the proper party as listed below:1234567891011121314151617181920212223 * * *(13) Senator in the General Assembly: Five hundred[.], except a sitting Senator in the General Assembly who currently holds the office for that district may instead file a certificate of nomination, on a form prescribed by the Secretary of the Commonwealth, on which the Senator informs the Secretary of the Commonwealth of the intent to seek reelection with their current party. If a Senator in the General Assembly files a certificate of nomination, the Senator may not challenge the nomination petition or nomination papers filed for that office and district. A person may not file a certificate of nomination under this paragraph for the office of Senator in the General Assembly in the election immediately following the redistricting of the Commonwealth. A certificate of nomination may not be posted on the Department of State's publicly accessible Internet website and may only be made available to a person who makes a request to the Secretary of the Commonwealth to examine the certificate of nomination.(14) Representative in the General Assembly: Three hundred[.], except a sitting Representative in the General Assembly who currently holds the office for that district may instead file a certificate of nomination, on a form prescribed by the Secretary of the Commonwealth, on which the Representative informs the Secretary of the Commonwealth of the intent to seek reelection with their current party. If a Representative in the General Assembly files a certificate of nomination, the Representative may not challenge the nomination petition or nomination papers filed for that office and district. A person may not file a certificate of nomination under this paragraph for the office of Representative in the 20250HB1898PN2368 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 General Assembly in the election immediately following the redistricting of the Commonwealth. A certificate of nomination shall not be posted on the Department of State's publicly accessible Internet website and may only be made available to a person who makes a request to the Secretary of the Commonwealth to examine the certificate of nomination.* * *Section 2. This act shall take effect in 60 days.20250HB1898PN2368 - 3 - 12345678
