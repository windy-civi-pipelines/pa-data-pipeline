2025-2026 Regular Session HB 405 PN 0377 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 377 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE BILL No.405 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY MARKOSEK, HILL-EVANS, HADDOCK, WEBSTER, McNEILL, SANCHEZ, GIRAL, HARKINS, DONAHUE, SCHLOSSBERG, CIRESI, FIEDLER, DEASY, GREEN AND CEPEDA-FREYTIZ, JANUARY 29, 2025 REFERRED TO COMMITTEE ON STATE GOVERNMENT, JANUARY 29, 2025 AN ACTAmending Title 15 (Corporations and Unincorporated Associations) of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes, in general provisions relating to business corporations, providing for eligibility for receipt of State resources.The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:Section 1. Title 15 of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes is amended by adding a section to read:ยง 1111. Eligibility for receipt of State resources.(a) Conditions.--A domestic business corporation or a foreign business corporation with 200 or more employees in this Commonwealth shall not be eligible for a State contract, State funding or a State grant if the corporation:(1) has violated 29 U.S.C. Ch. 8 (relating to fair labor standards) within the previous five years;(2) has been found guilty of an unfair labor practice in this Commonwealth within the previous five years;(3) has violated the act of August 15, 1961 (P.L.987, 123456789101112131415161718 No.442), known as the Pennsylvania Prevailing Wage Act, within the previous five years; or(4) fails to submit to the Department of State current bylaws of the corporation, which must provide for a corporation having a governing board, with at least 20% of the members of the governing board of the corporation being current employees of the corporation and elected by the employees of the corporation.(b) Applicability.--This section does not apply if the condition described in subsection (a)(1), (2) or (3) occurred prior to the effective date of this subsection.Section 2. The addition of 15 Pa.C.S. ยง 1111 shall apply to the following:(1) A State contract entered into or renewed on or after the effective date of this section.(2) State funding approved or otherwise made on or after the effective date of this section.(3) A State grant approved on or after the effective date of this section.Section 3. This act shall take effect in 60 days.20250HB0405PN0377 - 2 - 1234567891011121314151617181920
