2025-2026 Regular Session HR 226 PN 1583 Bill Text (HTM) See other bills under thesame topic PRINTER'S NO. 1583 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF PENNSYLVANIAHOUSE RESOLUTION No.226 Session of 2025 INTRODUCED BY BELLMON, MAY 5, 2025 INTRODUCED AS NONCONTROVERSIAL RESOLUTION UNDER RULE 35, MAY 5, 2025A RESOLUTIONHonoring the memory of the Honorable Isabella V. "Izzy" Fitzgerald, a former member of the House of Representatives, who died December 6, 2024.WHEREAS, Ms. Fitzgerald faithfully represented the 203rd District, Northwest Philadelphia, Philadelphia County, as a member of the General Assembly from 2017 through 2023; andWHEREAS, Ms. Fitzgerald notably contributed to the Legislative Black Caucus, the Philadelphia Delegation and PennSERVE; andWHEREAS, In addition to her terms as a lawmaker, Ms. Fitzgerald's legacy of service to the House of Representatives includes her work as a legislative assistant to her predecessor, Representative Dwight Evans; andWHEREAS, From 2000 through 2002, Ms. Fitzgerald was a special assistant to United States Representative Robert A. Brady; andWHEREAS, Ms. Fitzgerald has earned further recognition as a community organizer and ward leader; andWHEREAS, A Philadelphia native, born December 9, 1949, Ms. 123456789101112131415161718 Fitzgerald was a graduate of Overbrook High School and a longtime member of Enon Tabernacle Baptist Church and the West Oak Lane community; andWHEREAS, Ms. Fitzgerald's advocacy and vision for cleaner, safer communities extended to the Mount Airy and East Oak Lane neighborhoods; andWHEREAS, Ms. Fitzgerald fought for the benefit of children and families in her long association with the Northwest CommUnity Coalition for Youth; andWHEREAS, Through this nonprofit organization, Ms. Fitzgerald supported community leaders who built and sustained several key partnerships among parents, schools, agencies, faith-based institutions and other community-based groups; andWHEREAS, For more than a decade, Ms. Fitzgerald dedicated her energies to the diagnosis, treatment and spiritual and emotional support of women with breast cancer as a board member of Women of Faith and Hope; andWHEREAS, By a City of Philadelphia resolution introduced in September 2024, the City of Philadelphia renamed Stenton Avenue and Washington Lane in Ms. Fitzgerald's honor on December 15, 2024; andWHEREAS, The purpose of the renaming is to honor Ms. Fitzgerald's visionary leadership, tireless dedication and significant impact on the community and serve as a lasting tribute to her legacy and her guiding principle, "Let's Keep Moving Forward"; andWHEREAS, On December 18, 2024, after a funeral service at her church, Ms. Fitzgerald was interred at Philadelphia's Ivy Hill Cemetery; andWHEREAS, The House of Representatives expresses gratitude for 20250HR0226PN1583 - 2 - 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930 Ms. Fitzgerald's generous community outreach; andWHEREAS, The House of Representatives pays tribute to the consistency and continuity of Ms. Fitzgerald's leadership; therefore be itRESOLVED, That the House of Representatives honor the memory of the Honorable Isabella V. "Izzy" Fitzgerald, a former member of the House of Representatives, who died December 6, 2024; and be it furtherRESOLVED, That a copy of this resolution be transmitted to Ms. Fitzgerald's family in care of her sons Ray and Derek Fitzgerald.20250HR0226PN1583 - 3 - 1234567891011
